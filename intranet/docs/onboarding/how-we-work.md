# How We Work

## The Client Journey

Every client engagement follows a 7-stage journey. Not every stage applies to every client, but the sequence is consistent.

| Stage | Name | What Happens |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Recognition** | They find us. Toolkit, content, referral. |
| 2 | **Conversation** | Informal discovery. Mutual fit assessment. |
| 3 | **Mirror** | 90-minute facilitated session. Listen and reflect. |
| 4 | **Map** | Co-design the engagement. Scope, timeline, pricing. |
| 5 | **Integration** | The core work. Facilitation, filming, data collection. |
| 6 | **Story Travels** | Post-production. Editing, reports, final delivery. |
| 7 | **Return** | Offboarding. Gratitude. The door stays open. |

For full SOPs at each stage, see [Engagement Lifecycle](../operations/engagement-lifecycle.md).

Four phases. The pace varies; the sequence doesn't.

## Communication Style

We communicate the way we facilitate: directly, warmly, honestly.

Every client interaction should model the tone we're asking clients to practice. No jargon. No hedging. No marketing speak. Clear next steps in every message.

See [Client Communication Standards](../operations/client-communication.md) for templates and protocols.

## Decision-Making

Two principles govern all decisions:

**Sacral authority.** Gut response over cognitive override. If Isaac is thinking himself into a yes, it's a no. This applies to taking projects, pricing decisions, and scope changes.

**Protect the energy.** Overcommitment degrades everything. Max 2 Territory clients, max 4 Map engagements per quarter, no calls before noon on deep work days.

See [Decision Frameworks](../operations/decision-frameworks.md) for the full protocol.

## The Generator Rhythm

RP operates on a Generator rhythm:

- **Wait for response.** Don't chase. The right work arrives.
- **5-day latency.** Creative deliverables get 5 days of integration time before delivery. This is non-negotiable.
- **Energy check.** If weekly energy is below 6/10, no new commitments.
- **Deep work mornings.** No calls before noon, minimum 3 days per week.

These aren't preferences — they're operational architecture.
