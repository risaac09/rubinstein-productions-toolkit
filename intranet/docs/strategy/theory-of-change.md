# Theory of Change

## The Causal Pathway

```
Narrative Dysregulation → Facilitated Excavation → Authentic Articulation → Amplified Expression → Organizational Coherence
```

## The Problem

Mission-driven organizations suffer from Narrative Dysregulation: a structural gap between their operational reality (what they do) and their expressed identity (what they say). This gap creates downstream failures in fundraising, team alignment, public trust, and leadership transitions.

The symptoms are visible everywhere: jargon-heavy communications that say nothing, messaging that sounds like every other organization in the sector, leaders who are brilliant in private but wooden in public, teams that execute well but can't explain why the work matters.

## The Root Cause

Narrative Dysregulation is not a communications problem. It is a safety problem. Organizations cannot say what's true because the institutional environment has made honesty feel dangerous. The gap persists because:

1. **Performance pressure** rewards polished messaging over authentic expression
2. **Institutional memory** carries unprocessed conflict and fear
3. **Power dynamics** suppress the voices closest to the actual work
4. **Measurement systems** only track what can be counted, not what matters

## The Intervention

Rubinstein Productions intervenes at the point of safety, not messaging. The methodology creates conditions where honest expression becomes possible again by:

1. **Container** — Establishing nervous-system safety through co-regulation, making the interview or facilitation space feel fundamentally different from the institutional environment
2. **Excavation** — Listening for what's alive beneath the official narrative, using the camera as a co-regulatory witness rather than an extractive device
3. **Performance** — Shipping cameras to participants so they film on their own terms, in their own spaces, without a crew
4. **Translation** — Editing and returning materials so the story travels without the facilitator

## The Mechanism

The methodology works because:

- Nervous-system safety produces narrative coherence (Porges, 2011)
- Self-authored expression increases stakeholder buy-in (Self-Authorship Theory)
- Measuring in two registers (Royal + Nomadic) captures both institutional accountability and relational transformation
- Returning all materials to the client builds trust and eliminates extractive dynamics

## The Outcomes

**Short-term (engagement period):**
Participants produce authentic narrative artifacts. The organization sees itself more clearly. The Bilingual Dashboard documents both measurable outcomes and emergent shifts.

**Medium-term (3–12 months post-engagement):**
Increased narrative coherence in public communications. Improved fundraising language. Stronger internal alignment between leadership and front-line staff.

**Long-term (1–3 years):**
Organizational culture shifts toward honest expression as a norm. The tools and frameworks become self-sustaining without the facilitator.

## Grant-Facing Application

This Theory of Change maps directly to funder evaluation frameworks. The Royal Metrics satisfy standard logic-model requirements (inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes). The Nomadic Indicators provide the qualitative depth that distinguishes strong applications from generic ones.

For grant proposals, lead with the Royal pathway and use Nomadic Indicators as the "what makes this different" section. The Bilingual Dashboard is the evaluation framework — it satisfies institutional accountability while demonstrating methodological sophistication.
