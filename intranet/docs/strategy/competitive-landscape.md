## Competitive Landscape

### What Exists

| Competitor Type | What They Do | Where They Fall Short |
|----------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Communications consultants** | Messaging frameworks, brand strategy, media training | Start with message, not with safety. Products are polished but inauthentic. |
| **Documentary filmmakers** | Beautiful films, compelling narratives | Extractive model — crew comes in, shoots, leaves. Subjects have no authorship. |
| **Organizational consultants** | Strategic planning, change management | No production capability. Can diagnose but can't create expressive artifacts. |
| **DEI consultants** | Equity audits, training programs | Often compliance-oriented. Don't address narrative as a systemic issue. |
| **Storytelling agencies** | Impact videos, case studies, annual report content | Transactional. "Tell us your story" without creating conditions for truth. |

### The RP Difference

No one else combines all three:

1. **Facilitation methodology** rooted in nervous-system science (not just "great interviewing")
2. **Production capability** with a participant-led model (camera ships to them, not crew)
3. **Bilingual measurement** that speaks both institutional and relational languages

The closest competitors address one of these three. RP addresses all three as an integrated practice.

## Pricing Context

RP pricing ($1,500 entry to $8,000/mo retainer) sits below major consultancies ($25K+/engagement) but above freelance videographers ($500–$2,000/project). The positioning justifies the premium over production-only services: clients are paying for methodology + facilitation + measurement, not just footage.

The risk is being perceived as "expensive videography" rather than "facilitation that includes production." Positioning language must lead with the methodology and the Bilingual Dashboard, not with the camera.
