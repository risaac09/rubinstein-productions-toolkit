# Brand Identity

## Brand Essence

**Tagline:** *The liver processes and expresses.*

This is both a metaphor and a methodology statement. The liver doesn't decide what to filter — it processes what arrives. Similarly, RP doesn't prescribe a message — it creates conditions for authentic expression to emerge from what's already there.

## Visual Identity

### Colors

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Marrow** | `#1a1412` | Primary dark. Backgrounds, headers, body text. |
| **Blood** | `#8b2e1c` | Secondary accent. CTAs, H2 headings, active states. |
| **Amber** | `#c8923a` | Warm accent. Dividers, highlights, progress indicators. |
| **Bone** | `#f2ece4` | Light background. Cards, table alternating rows. |
| **Ash** | `#a89e93` | Muted text. Captions, metadata, secondary info. |
| **White** | `#faf8f5` | Page background. Warm white, not clinical. |

### Typography

| Context | Font | Fallback | Usage |
|---------|------|----------|-------|
| Headlines | EB Garamond | Georgia | Italic, warm, literary |
| Body | Inter | Calibri | Clean, readable, professional |
| Code/monospace | Geist Mono | Consolas | Terminal output, CLI references |

### Design Principles

- **Warm, not clinical.** Everything should feel like a well-lit room, not a hospital. Bone and White backgrounds, never pure white (#fff).
- **Serif headlines, sans body.** The tension between literary (Georgia) and functional (Calibri) mirrors the RP duality — Nomadic soul, Royal execution.
- **Amber as accent, never dominant.** Amber marks transitions, progress, and warmth. It should draw the eye without overwhelming.
- **Marrow for authority.** Dark backgrounds signal seriousness and depth. Use for hero sections and key statements.
- **Ash for secondary.** Metadata, timestamps, attributions, footer content.

## Voice Guidelines

### Tone

Direct. Warm. Honest. Never performative.

Write like you're talking to someone who is smart and busy and deserves your respect. No jargon that hasn't been earned through explanation. No hedging. No marketing speak.

### Voice Rules

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Lead with craft and curiosity | Lead with ideology or diagnosis |
| Say "I" when it's Isaac's perspective | Hide behind "we" or passive voice |
| Name the specific methodology | Use vague claims ("transformative," "innovative") |
| Acknowledge what's hard | Minimize difficulty or overpromise |
| End with a concrete next step | End with inspirational abstraction |

### Sample Voice

**Yes:** "I facilitate. I ship a camera to the participant. They film themselves. Everything is returned."

**No:** "We leverage innovative narrative frameworks to empower organizational stakeholders through transformative storytelling experiences."

**Yes:** "The methodology works because nervous-system safety produces narrative coherence."

**No:** "Our holistic approach creates synergies between authentic expression and strategic communication."

## Brand Context for AI Tools

When using AI tools (Claude Code skills, prompt stack), the brand context file should include:

- The liver metaphor and what it means
- The Bilingual Dashboard concept (Royal + Nomadic)
- The four-phase methodology (Container → Excavation → Performance → Translation)
- The "not a videographer" distinction
- Pricing tiers and floor rates
- Voice guidelines above

This ensures every AI-generated output sounds like RP, not like a generic consultancy.
