# R&D / Innovation

The innovation department is not where novelty gets chased. It's where the **originality factor** is protected, refined, and expanded.

Most creative studios have no R&D. They have hustle. The difference: hustle chases what's working in the market right now. R&D asks what the work wants to become next, and runs small bets to find out.

RP's R&D has four loops:

1. **Methodology R&D** — how facilitation, film, and nervous-system practice integrate (and where they should diverge)
2. **Tooling R&D** — what new skills, agents, or stack moves increase practice leverage
3. **Product R&D** — what gets packaged and sold (Metabolizer is the first; more will come)
4. **Creative research** — what's being watched, read, studied, absorbed

Every loop is governed by one constraint: **the originality factor**. If an experiment violates it, the experiment fails by definition.

---

## Charter

R&D exists to:

1. **Protect the core.** See [originality-factor.md](originality-factor.md). The non-negotiables.
2. **Expand the adjacent.** Methodology, tools, products evolve — within the constraint of recognizably being RP's work.
3. **Kill the dead.** Experiments that don't work stop. Fast. No life support.
4. **Canonize what works.** Successful experiments become documented methodology and flow back to Executive / Delivery.

---

## Operating principles

**One experiment at a time, per loop.** Four concurrent experiments maximum (one per loop). More than that is scatter.

**Every experiment has a kill criterion.** Before the experiment starts, define what failure looks like. "I'll just see what happens" is not R&D. It's procrastination.

**Timebox.** Default: 4 weeks. If it needs longer, name why.

**Pilot with one real client, not a hypothetical.** Theory is cheap. The moment of truth is someone actually paying for or using the thing.

**Publish the post-mortem.** Whether the experiment succeeded or failed, write what was learned. Kept in `experiments.md`.

## Governance

**Sponsor:** Isaac (sole). R&D is a personal commitment, not a budget line.

**Budget:** Time-boxed, not dollar-boxed. 1 half-day per week is the baseline R&D allocation.

**Review cadence:** Monthly. At month-end, look at active experiments, kill or continue, name what was learned.

**Canonization path:** Experiment → pilot → writeup → update to `learning/` or `operations/` doc → new skill or agent if warranted.

## Relationship to other departments

| Department | Flow |
|---|---|
| **Executive** | Receives: methodology updates that should shape positioning. Sends: constraints (what RP is for). |
| **Delivery** | Receives: new formats to pilot. Sends: friction signals from client work ("this keeps breaking"). |
| **Marketing** | Receives: new language, new formats worth publishing. Sends: audience signals. |
| **Finance** | Sends: budget constraints. Receives: pricing signals for new products. |
| **IT** | Partners on tooling R&D. Every new agent or skill is co-owned. |

---

## What R&D is **not**

- **Not ideation.** Ideas without kill criteria and pilots are journal entries, not experiments.
- **Not a side project repo.** If a side project has no path back to the practice, it lives elsewhere (personal GitHub, scratch).
- **Not where featuritis lives.** Adding capability to existing products is Product/Delivery work. R&D proposes new directions.
- **Not generic creative industry trend-watching.** Research is targeted at the originality factor, not at mimicking what's working for other studios.
