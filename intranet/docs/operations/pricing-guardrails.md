## Hard Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Floor rate** | $150/hr. Do not work below this under any framing. |
| **Session minimum** | $500. No exceptions. |
| **Video minimum** | $2,000. No exceptions. |
| **Anchor high** | Always present the highest applicable tier first. Negotiate down, never up. |
| **Scoping is labor** | A "small ask" from a large org is still Tier 2 pricing. |

## Discount Policy

Discounts are available only in these specific circumstances:

| Circumstance | Maximum Discount | Documentation Required |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Aligned nonprofits (mission match + budget constraint) | 30% | Written justification in project notes |
| Barter with documented value exchange | Equivalent value | Written agreement specifying exchange |
| Phase 1 beta clients (building case studies) | Negotiable, above floor | Documented as beta, not repeated |

!!! warning "Discount Discipline"
    Every discount must be documented with a reason. If you find yourself discounting more than 20% of engagements, pricing or positioning needs adjustment — not your generosity.

## Pricing Pushback Protocol

When a client says "that's too expensive":

1. **Do NOT immediately discount.** Pause.
2. **Gather information:** "What budget are you working with?" (understand the gap)
3. **If near floor:** Offer reduced scope, not reduced rate. "I can do X within that budget instead of Y."
4. **If far below floor:** "I appreciate the interest — that's below my minimum engagement. I can refer you to someone else, or we can revisit when budget aligns."
5. **If strategic relationship:** Maximum 20% discount. Document it. Do not repeat it with the same client.

### What Not to Say

- "I can make that work" (when you can't)
- "Let me see what I can do" (opens the door to arbitrary cuts)
- "For you, I'll make an exception" (creates precedent)

### What to Say

- "Here's what I can offer within that range..."
- "The Mirror session might be the right starting point for your budget."
- "I'd rather do less work at the right rate than more work at a rate that compromises quality."
