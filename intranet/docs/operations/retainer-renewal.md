# Retainer Renewal — Territory Tier

Territory engagements (the $4–8k/mo retainer tier) imply ongoing relationship. Without a deliberate renewal conversation, retainers default to **drift** — either indefinite auto-renewal without fit-checking, or quiet non-renewal without a real close.

This doc is the cadence and the script shape for naming the renewal conversation on purpose.

---

## Cadence

| Month of retainer | Action |
|---|---|
| Month 1 | Calibration check at week 4: is the shape right? |
| Month 3 | First fit review — still the right work, still the right rhythm? |
| Month 6 | Mid-point reflection — what's shifted for the client, what should shift in the work |
| Month 10 | **Renewal conversation scheduled.** Two months' notice. |
| Month 12 | Renewal decision enacted (renew, re-scope, or close cleanly) |

**Two-month notice is the rule.** A renewal conversation at month 12 is too late — it traps both parties into a yes-or-no with no runway to reshape.

---

## The renewal conversation (month 10)

A real conversation, not a pricing email. 45 min.

### What it is not
- A sales call
- A performance review of the work
- A justification of the next year's fee

### What it is
A joint assessment of whether the continued work is still the right work. Structured around three questions:

1. **What shifted in the last 12 months** — for the client, for their organization, for the conversation between you.
2. **What's still unmetabolized** — the thing the retainer was working on that's still open, or the new thing that has surfaced that wasn't there at signing.
3. **What shape fits the next chapter** — same retainer, reshaped retainer, or a clean close + a different engagement (e.g., back to Map-tier project work).

### Possible outcomes

| Outcome | When |
|---|---|
| **Renew as-is** | The work is still the work. Rare — most retainers should evolve. |
| **Re-scope and renew** | The work has changed; the retainer shape should change to match. Most common. |
| **Close cleanly, re-engage later** | The ongoing rhythm is no longer the right shape. Offer explicit warm closure + open door. |
| **Close and part** | Fit has eroded. Say so. Don't drift. |

---

## Pricing decisions at renewal

- **Default:** hold price. Don't raise by default, don't discount by default.
- **Raise:** if scope has genuinely expanded, or if the relationship is sitting below the tier band and restoring it to market.
- **Lower / re-scope down:** if the retainer has become lighter-touch but still valued. Better to right-size than to inflate.
- **Never:** surprise-raise at renewal. If a raise is coming, it is named in the month-10 conversation, not the month-12 contract.

See `pricing-guardrails.md` for band edges.

---

## Clean closure practice

Not-renewing is a form of respect, not a failure. The post-closure rhythm:
- Send a written closure note — what the work accomplished, what was left open, what door stays open.
- Move contact `status` to `past-client` in contacts.json.
- Schedule a 6-month and 12-month stewardship touchpoint (independent of new-work pursuit).

Past clients are the single most productive referral source. The renewal conversation is the entry point to that long rhythm.
