## Hard Limits

| Constraint | Limit | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Active Territory partnerships | Max 2 | These are deep, ongoing relationships. More than 2 degrades quality. |
| Map engagements per quarter | Max 4 | Each sprint consumes 3–5 days of intensive work plus prep and follow-up. |
| Mirror sessions | Unlimited | But must not displace deep work blocks. |
| Deep work mornings | Minimum 3/week | No calls before noon. Protect creative and analytical capacity. |
| Filming sessions | Requires 24hr prep + 5-day post-latency | Block these in advance. They consume more energy than they appear to. |

## Capacity Assessment Protocol

When a new opportunity arrives, run this checklist:

### Step 1: Current Load

| Question | Check |
|----------|-------|
| How many Territory clients are active? | Must be < 2 |
| How many Map engagements are in-flight this quarter? | Must be < 4 |
| Are all current client deadlines on track? | Must be yes |

### Step 2: Energy Check

| Question | Check |
|----------|-------|
| What do the next 4 weeks look like energetically? | No red flags |
| Does this project require filming? | If yes, add 6+ days to workload estimate |
| Is weekly energy above 6/10? | If below 6, no new commitments |

### Step 3: Sacral Check

| Question | Check |
|----------|-------|
| Is this a "hell yes" or a "probably"? | Gut response, not cognitive override |
| Are you thinking yourself into saying yes? | If yes, it's probably a no |
| Will taking this compromise current client quality? | Must be no |

### Decision

If **3 or more flags are yellow** → recommend deferring or scoping down.

If **energy is below 6/10** → no new commitments, no exceptions.

## Calendar Architecture

### Weekly Template

| Block | Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Morning (8–12) | Deep work | Deep work | Deep work | Flexible | Deep work |
| Afternoon (1–5) | Client calls | Production | Client calls | Admin/finance | Free |

### Rules

- **No calls before noon** on deep work days
- **Client sessions:** Tuesdays and Thursdays preferred
- **Admin/finance:** Thursday afternoons (invoicing, pipeline review, follow-ups)
- **Fridays:** Flexible — use for overflow, learning, or rest
- **Filming days:** Block the full day plus the following day for processing

### Seasonal Patterns

- **Q1 (Jan–Mar):** Grant season. Budget time for RWJF, other applications.
- **Q2 (Apr–Jun):** Conference season. Speaking opportunities, networking.
- **Q3 (Jul–Sep):** Production heavy. Longer days, outdoor filming feasible.
- **Q4 (Oct–Dec):** Year-end. Renewals, annual reports, case study writing.

## Overcommitment Recovery Protocol

If capacity is exceeded (it will happen):

1. **Acknowledge it.** Name it to yourself and to any affected clients.
2. **Triage.** Which commitments are most time-sensitive? Which can be renegotiated?
3. **Communicate early.** "I'm at capacity and want to make sure your work gets the attention it deserves. Can we adjust the timeline by [X]?"
4. **Protect the 5-day latency.** This is the first thing to get cut when overcommitted — don't let it. The quality of creative deliverables depends on it.
5. **Post-mortem.** What led to overcommitment? Was it a Sacral override? A pricing issue that made you take too many small jobs? Adjust the intake process.
