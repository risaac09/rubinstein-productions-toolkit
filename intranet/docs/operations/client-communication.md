# Client Communication Standards

How we communicate is part of the methodology. Every interaction should model the honest, direct, warm tone we're asking clients to practice.

---

## Response Time Standards

| Context | Target |
|---------|--------|
| New prospect inquiry | Within 48 hours |
| Active client email | Within 24 hours (business days) |
| Urgent issue (filming day, tech problem) | Same day |
| Post-session follow-up | Within 24 hours |
| Mirror Narrative delivery | 5–7 days after session (never before 5) |
| Proposal delivery | Within 3 business days of scoping call |

---

## Communication Principles

**Be direct.** If something isn't working, name it. If the timeline is slipping, say so before it becomes a problem. Clients respect honesty more than reassurance.

**Be brief.** Every email should be readable in under 2 minutes. If it needs more, it should be a document or a call.

**Match their channel.** If they email, email back. If they text, text back. Don't force a call when a message will do. Don't send a long email when a quick text is appropriate.

**Name the next step.** Every client-facing communication should end with a clear next action — who does what by when.

## Email Templates

### After a Conversation (Stage 2)

Subject: Good talking with you

> [Name] — Thanks for the conversation today. What I heard was [1-sentence summary of their situation]. I think a Mirror Session could be useful here.
>
> If you'd like to proceed, here are a few times that work: [options]. No prep needed on your end — I'll send a brief guide beforehand.
>
> Isaac

### Sending the Mirror Narrative (Stage 3)

Subject: Your Mirror Narrative

> [Name] — Attached is the Mirror Narrative from our session on [date].
>
> This document is yours to keep regardless of what happens next. Take your time with it — it's meant to be sat with, not speed-read.
>
> When you're ready, I'd welcome a conversation about what landed and what didn't. No rush.
>
> Isaac

### Sending a Proposal (Stage 4)

Subject: Engagement proposal — [Project Name]

> [Name] — Following up on our conversation, I've put together a proposal and project roadmap. Both are attached.
>
> The short version: [1-sentence summary of scope]. Total investment: [amount]. Timeline: [duration].
>
> Happy to walk through it on a call if helpful, or feel free to review and send questions my way. The proposal is valid for 30 days.
>
> Isaac

### Mid-Engagement Check-In (Stage 5)

Subject: Check-in — [Project Name]

> [Name] — Quick update on where we are.
>
> [2-3 sentences on progress, what's working, anything that needs attention]
>
> Attached is a more detailed Progress Check-In with the Bilingual Dashboard snapshot.
>
> One thing I'd like your input on: [specific question or decision point].
>
> Isaac

### Thank You / Offboarding (Stage 7)

Subject: Thank you

> [Name] — [personalized opening referencing something specific from the engagement].
>
> All deliverables have been transferred. Full details are in the attached offboarding letter.
>
> [1-2 sentences of sustainability advice].
>
> If you know someone doing similar work, I'd welcome the introduction. And if something comes up in six months — a question about the footage, a new initiative — my door stays open.
>
> With gratitude,
> Isaac

## Difficult Conversations

### Client wants scope changes mid-engagement

"I hear you — that's a reasonable thing to want. Here's what I'd suggest: we complete the current scope as planned, and I'll include a section in the final delivery about how [new request] could be addressed in a follow-up engagement. That way you get the full value of what we've already scoped without diluting either effort."

### Client is unhappy with a deliverable

"Thank you for being direct about this — that's exactly what I ask of my clients. Let me understand what isn't landing. [Listen.] Here's what I'd propose for the revision..."

### Client ghosts after proposal

Wait 7 days, then one follow-up: "Just circling back on the proposal. No rush — happy to adjust scope or timing if anything shifted on your end. If the timing isn't right, that's completely fine too."

If no response after 14 days, archive. Do not follow up again.
