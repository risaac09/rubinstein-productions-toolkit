## Opportunity Triage (A/B/C)

When any new opportunity arrives, classify it immediately:

### A — Aligned

Core RP methodology, right avatar, respects pricing, energetically clear.

**Action:** Proceed. Route to proposal.

### B — Adjacent

Good client, wrong scope, could be shaped. Or: right work, budget doesn't quite fit.

**Action:** Negotiate scope. Hold pricing floor. Set clear delivery boundaries. If it can be shaped into alignment, proceed.

### C — Misaligned

Wrong avatar, values mismatch, below floor rate, scope creep risk, or cognitive override ("I *should* take this").

**Action:** Decline gracefully. Offer a referral if possible. Do not negotiate.

## Client Acceptance Filter

Six questions. Score yes/no.

| # | Question | Y/N |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | Sacral yes? (gut response, not mental reasoning) | |
| 2 | Under capacity? (see [Capacity Management](capacity-management.md)) | |
| 3 | Price meets minimums? (see [Pricing Guardrails](pricing-guardrails.md)) | |
| 4 | Mission-aligned or clear bridge income? | |
| 5 | Builds desired portfolio? (case study value, sector entry, relationship) | |
| 6 | Deliverable without stress? (scope is clear, timeline is realistic) | |

**Scoring:**

- 5–6 YES → Take it
- 3–4 YES → Flag concerns, proceed with conditions
- 0–2 YES → Decline

## "Should I Take This?" Quick Protocol

When you're staring at an opportunity and can't decide:

1. **Classify** it (A/B/C above)
2. **Check capacity** (current load — Territory count, Map count, energy level)
3. **Sacral check:** Does this feel like a "hell yes" or a "probably"? If you're *thinking* yourself into yes, it's probably a no.
4. **Present the answer:**
    - **A:** Take it
    - **B:** Counter-propose (different scope, timeline, or price)
    - **C:** Pass

!!! tip "The Cognitive Override Test"
    If you catch yourself building a case for why you *should* take something — listing reasons, rationalizing the discount, imagining the upside — that's cognitive override, not Sacral response. The body knows before the mind constructs a story. Trust the first signal.

## Scope Creep Detection

Signs that scope is drifting mid-engagement:

| Signal | What It Looks Like |
|--------|-------------------|
| "While we're at it..." | Client adds deliverables conversationally |
| Meeting frequency increases | Calls get longer or more frequent without plan |
| Deliverable list grows | Original proposal referenced 3 things, you're now doing 5 |
| New stakeholders appear | People not in the original scope start requesting things |
| "Quick question" frequency | Small asks that collectively consume hours |

### Response Protocol

1. **Name it early:** "I want to flag that we're moving beyond the original scope. That's not a problem, but I want to be transparent about it."
2. **Reference the proposal:** "The engagement we scoped covers X, Y, Z. What you're describing is additional. I can absolutely do it — here's what it would look like as an add-on."
3. **Price the add-on:** Even if it's small, price it. Free additions train clients to expect free additions.
4. **Document the change:** Send a brief email confirming the scope adjustment and any pricing change.

## Generator Energy Principles (Hard Rules)

These override all other decision frameworks.

| Principle | What It Means |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Wait for response** | Do not chase opportunities that require convincing. The right work comes to you. |
| **Sacral authority** | Gut response over cognitive override. If you're thinking yourself into yes, it's a no. |
| **5-day latency** | Never deliver or decide on creative work within 5 days of production. Enforce this in all timelines. |
| **Sustainable commitment** | Overcommitment is self-betrayal. The COO function exists to prevent this. |
| **Revenue ≠ alignment** | A high-paying misaligned project costs more than it earns in energy, reputation, and opportunity cost. |

## Quarterly Review Protocol

Run at the start of each quarter (January, April, July, October):

1. **Revenue review:** Actual vs. target by tier. Tax reserve status.
2. **Capacity review:** Was Generator energy protected? What drained vs. energized?
3. **Pricing review:** Any below-floor deals? Pattern in pushback? Adjustment needed?
4. **Methodology review:** What worked in the Bilingual Dashboard? What needs refinement?
5. **Pipeline review:** Top 5 prospects for next quarter. Warm relationships to re-engage.
6. **Phase check:** Where are we on the [roadmap](../strategy/phase-roadmap.md)? What's the next milestone?

Output: 1-page quarterly memo saved to Obsidian `Practice/Rubinstein Productions/`.
