# Delegation Signals

RP does not hire on a revenue threshold or a retainer-count threshold. It hires when **capacity signals** cross specific lines that indicate the instrument is being worn, not used.

The shape of delegation is deliberately unfixed (VA, sub-contractor, collaborator, contract editor). What's fixed is the trigger criteria.

---

## Hard signals (any one fires → delegation conversation within 2 weeks)

| Signal | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Late-night work sessions | 3+ per week, 2 weeks running |
| Missed weekly day off | 2+ times per quarter |
| Sabbatical cancelled or compressed | Once (any quarter) |
| Sleep under 6.5 hrs averaged | 2 weeks running (see `activities.json`) |
| Client session cancelled by Isaac | Twice within a month for non-illness reasons |
| "I'm behind" as recurring internal narrative | Named 3+ weeks in a row in friction log / journal |

**These are not aspirational standards.** They are pre-committed tripwires. If one fires, the delegation conversation happens — even if the revenue doesn't yet feel like it supports a hire.

---

## Soft signals (watch for clustering)

- Admin work displacing thinking work more than 2 days/week
- Content publishing slipping off the weekly rhythm for 3+ weeks
- Responding to client emails feels heavy before opening them
- Saying yes to engagements that would normally get qualified out
- Inner-practice log going quiet

**Rule:** if 2+ soft signals are present for 3+ weeks, treat it as one hard signal fire.

---

## What delegation looks like at first fire

Not a full-time hire. Start with the narrowest intervention that relieves the signal:

| Signal | First-move delegation |
|---|---|
| Admin displacement | VA for 5 hrs/week (inbox triage, scheduling, logistics) |
| Post-production load | Contract editor for specific deliverables |
| Client comms overwhelm | VA handling first-touch scheduling only |
| Research/brief work piling up | Contract researcher per-project |
| Content production slipping | Contract editor + scheduler for 8-week commitment |

**Default:** narrow-scope, time-bounded, replaceable. Not "an assistant." A specific function delegated for a specific relief.

---

## What delegation does NOT look like

- Hiring to grow revenue (growth is not the trigger; depletion is)
- Hiring to take on more clients (that's a different conversation — capacity expansion, not relief)
- Hiring someone to facilitate, film, or write in RP's voice (these cannot be delegated in a solo practice)
- Hiring full-time on the first delegation

---

## The review cadence

- **Weekly:** quick self-check against the signal list (end of week, 2 min)
- **Monthly:** review activities.json for sleep / late-night / sabbatical adherence
- **Quarterly:** in the retainer renewal review period, also review delegation signal fires — has anything been chronic?

**Log delegation signal fires** in the Friction Log (inner-practice) or a dedicated section in stack-data. The pattern matters more than any single fire.

---

## Pre-committed language

When a signal fires, the conversation with self is:

> "Signal X fired. The question is not whether to hire. The question is which narrow function to delegate for 60 days to relieve it. What's the smallest intervention?"

Not: *"Can I afford it?"* Not: *"Am I ready?"* Not: *"Should I push through?"*

The tripwire exists precisely because those questions produce the wrong answer when the instrument is worn.

---

*"Delegate the function that's costing the instrument, not the function you wish you had."*
