# Sabbatical Policy

Three rest cadences, not one:

| Cadence | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly** | 1 day off (existing rule) | Nervous-system reset; prevent weekly accumulation |
| **Quarterly** | 1 week off | Integration — let a quarter's work settle before the next one begins |
| **Annual** | 1 month off | Structural rest — deep practice, deep rest, deep reorientation |

Rest is not absence of practice. It is a phase of it. See `people/index.md` charter item 3.

## Weekly day off

Already established. See `operations/capacity-management.md`. Unchanged by this doc.

## Quarterly week off

### Cadence
One week off per quarter. **Four weeks per year, scheduled a year in advance.**

Default placement (adjust to practice rhythm):
- Q1: mid-March
- Q2: mid-June
- Q3: mid-September
- Q4: late December (aligns with natural slowdown)

### Rules
- **Scheduled 12 months out.** Blocked in calendar as "RP closed" before any client work is committed against that window.
- **No client sessions, no content publishing, no proposal drafting.**
- **Admin-only is not acceptable.** The week is real rest, not catch-up.
- **One check-in email per day, maximum.** Urgent client comms only. No lurking in inbox.

### What the week is for
Not travel-by-default. The week is for whatever restores the instrument: trail time, reading, silence, unstructured days, visiting people, or literally nothing. Named in advance helps: *"this is my integration week."*

### Protocol
- **2 weeks before:** notify active clients with a short line about the closure window.
- **Day before:** set email auto-reply. Close all slack/comms loops.
- **Day after:** one morning of re-entry admin (2 hr max) before resuming normal load.

## Annual month off

### Cadence
One month off per year. **Scheduled 12+ months in advance.**

Default placement: **August** (Northeast summer, natural client slowdown, good running/travel weather).

### Rules
- **Scheduled before the fiscal year's client roster is built.** Revenue planning assumes 11 working months, not 12.
- **Full closure.** No client work, no content, no proposals. Check email 2x per week maximum for genuine emergencies.
- **Financial runway must cover it.** The 2–3 month reserve (see `financial-operations.md`) is prerequisite. If reserves drop below the annual-month threshold, sabbatical triggers a capacity conversation, not a cancellation.
- **Cannot be stacked with a quarterly week.** August sabbatical replaces the Q3 quarterly, it does not add to it.

### What the month is for
Named in advance each year. Examples:
- Pilgrimage / extended travel
- Deep reading / writing / research
- Race training block (for an A-race ultra)
- Silent retreat or structured practice intensive
- Unstructured — explicit permission to not have a purpose

**Name it a year out.** The shape of the rest matters; drifting into it produces anxiety, not integration.

### Client communication
- **6 weeks out:** email active clients with closure dates and handoff arrangements.
- **1 week out:** final check-in with each active engagement; any work needed during the month gets pre-negotiated or explicitly deferred.
- **Auto-reply:** names the return date, names who to contact (if anyone) for emergencies.

---

## Protection rules

These cadences are **not negotiable against client pressure**. A client who cannot accept pre-scheduled closure windows is not the right fit for a solo practice.

- If an Organizational Embedding retainer is proposed that conflicts with scheduled sabbatical, the retainer start date shifts or the retainer doesn't happen. Sabbatical does not.
- If revenue pressure tempts cancellation of the annual month, that is the signal to reread `capacity-management.md` and have a capacity conversation, not a cancellation conversation.

---

## The underlying stance

The instrument is the business. A depleted instrument produces depleted work. A rested instrument produces work that clients actually pay Organizational Embedding prices for.

The sabbatical cadence is not a cost center. It is the precondition for the quality that the pricing assumes.

*"Rest is what the work is made of."*
