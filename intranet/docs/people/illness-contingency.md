# Illness & Contingency Policy

In a solo practice, the instrument is the business. When Isaac is sick, injured, or otherwise capacity-compromised, client obligations do not pause themselves. This doc is the pre-decided protocol so that decisions don't get made inside the fog of being unwell.

The principle: **name the reality early, protect recovery, preserve trust.**

---

## Severity bands

| Band | Definition | Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Band 1 — minor** | 1–2 day disruption (cold, GI bug, minor strain) | Push non-client work; keep scheduled client commitments if genuinely able |
| **Band 2 — moderate** | 3–7 day disruption | Reschedule client sessions; protect a minimum-viable-delivery surface |
| **Band 3 — significant** | 1–3 week disruption | Trigger the communication protocol (below); reshape active engagements |
| **Band 4 — serious** | 3+ weeks, or unpredictable return | Trigger full contingency: client handoffs, refund conversations, potential pauses |

**Honesty rule:** err toward the higher band. Underestimating is a reflex and it costs trust later.

---

## Communication protocol (Band 2+)

### Day 1 of recognizing it's not Band 1
- [ ] Identify all clients with commitments in the next 14 days
- [ ] Draft one message per active client — short, direct, no over-explanation
- [ ] Send within 24 hours of the recognition (not within 24 hours of the first symptom)

### Template (adapt per relationship)
> I need to let you know I'm dealing with [brief, honest framing — no oversharing]. I expect to be at reduced capacity through approximately [date]. Here's what I can still hold and what I'd like to reschedule: [specifics]. I'll confirm updates by [date].

**What to include:** the reality, the timeline estimate, the specific impact on their work, the next checkpoint.
**What to skip:** medical detail, apology spirals, reassurances you can't keep.

---

## Reshape, don't cancel

For Band 2–3, default to **reshaping** active engagements rather than canceling:
- Replace a facilitation session with an async written deliverable
- Push the intensive phase by 2 weeks and deliver lighter-touch work in the interim
- Collapse a two-session week into one

Clients generally accept a reshape. They lose trust only when they're informed late or left guessing.

---

## Band 4 — when capacity won't return on a known timeline

- [ ] Within 72 hours of recognizing Band 4: schedule a call with each active retainer/Map client
- [ ] Offer explicit options: pause, refund a prorated amount, hand off to a named collaborator, close cleanly
- [ ] Do not negotiate from urgency. If decisions must wait until the fog lifts, say so.
- [ ] Notify the network-stewardship rhythm — past clients who are expecting stewardship touchpoints get a short holding note.

---

## Financial contingency

- **Runway check:** the practice should carry 2–3 months of expenses in reserve at all times (see `financial-operations.md`). This buys the space to recover without forced decisions.
- **Refunds:** prorate honestly. A refund issued quickly under Band 4 is the single most trust-preserving act available.
- **No PTO myth:** the solo practice has no PTO. Sabbatical rhythm (see `capacity-management.md`) is the structural answer — illness contingency is the acute answer.

---

## Recovery protocol

Returning to work after a Band 3–4 event is its own phase, not an on-off switch.
- **Week 1 back:** client communication only. No new work intake. No content publishing.
- **Week 2 back:** restart one active engagement at a time. Assess load at end of week.
- **Week 3 back:** return to regular rhythm only if Week 2 held.
- **Log it:** note the arc in `activities.json` + a private reflection — recovery patterns compound into self-knowledge.

---

## The underlying stance

Clients who respond poorly to honest, early, well-communicated illness updates are telling you something about the relationship. File that information.

*"The fastest path back to the instrument is the one that doesn't lie to itself about what the instrument needs."*
