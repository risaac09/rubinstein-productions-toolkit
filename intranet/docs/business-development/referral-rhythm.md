# Referral Rhythm — No-Fee Structured Stewardship

Past clients and trusted collaborators are RP's single most productive source of fit engagements. Unstructured, this happens randomly. Structured, it produces a dependable rhythm without turning into transactional sales.

**No referral fees.** Financial incentives introduce the wrong motive into relational stewardship. Referrals happen because someone sees the work clearly and wants to send someone toward it — or they don't.

---

## The quarterly rhythm

Four times per year, a specific outreach cycle to warm relationships:

### Quarter structure
- **Week 1 of quarter:** identify 8–12 people to reach out to (past clients, peer facilitators, collaborators). Mix: ~60% past clients, ~30% peers, ~10% dormant contacts.
- **Weeks 1–4:** send one outreach per business day. Not a blast. Individually written.
- **Week 5:** log responses + new introductions in `stack-data/data/contacts.json`.
- **Weeks 6–12:** steward any introductions that came through.

**Rule:** the outreach is a real touchpoint. Not "just checking in to see if you know anyone." It is a specific note about what the person is actually up to, with a clear offer of what RP is currently available for.

---

## The outreach shape

Two paragraphs. Three sentences each max.

**Paragraph 1 — a real observation about them.** What you've noticed in their work, their practice, their recent arc. Specific. No flattery.

**Paragraph 2 — where RP is now.** What kind of engagements are fitting this season, who RP is most useful to right now. End with: *"If anyone comes to mind, I'd love to know them."*

No P.S. No "no pressure." No follow-up sequence.

---

## Tracking

Minimal. In `contacts.json`:
- `lastStewardshipTouch`: date of last outreach
- `stewardshipCadence`: quarterly | biannual | annual | dormant
- Introductions tracked as new contacts with `source: referral-from-{contactId}`

**Metric that matters:** introductions received per quarter. Not conversions. Not closes. Just introductions — because the referrer did the work of seeing the fit.

**Counter-metric:** don't track "referral conversion rate." That framing corrupts the relationship.

---

## Peer-referral (facilitators, filmmakers, collaborators)

Separate rhythm from client stewardship, same principle:
- Refer to others generously when it's the right fit. A wrong-fit engagement handed off is a gift to both parties.
- Maintain a short list of 5–10 peer facilitators/filmmakers whose work you trust and can introduce clients to when RP isn't the right match.
- When a peer refers someone to RP: send a thank-you + a specific update when the engagement resolves (regardless of outcome).

---

## What this is NOT

- A commission structure
- A "partner program"
- An affiliate link system
- Anything that requires a contract or signup

It is a cadence. The structure is the calendar + the list, nothing more.

---

## Governance

- **Quarterly review** (end of each quarter): who responded, who went dormant, what introductions came through, what was the texture of the responses.
- **Annual review:** is the rhythm still producing fit introductions? Should the list composition shift? Should the cadence tighten or loosen?

---

*"The best referrals come from people who don't need incentive to make them."*
