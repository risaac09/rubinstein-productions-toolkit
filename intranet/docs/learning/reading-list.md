# Reading List & Intellectual Lineage

The RP methodology draws on several intellectual traditions. This is not required reading — it's the bibliography of the practice, organized by what each source contributes to the work.

---

## Nervous System & Co-Regulation

The foundation of the facilitation methodology.

- **Stephen Porges** — *The Polyvagal Theory* (2011). The scientific basis for why safety produces narrative coherence. Porges's work on the social engagement system explains why facilitator regulation entrains participant state.
- **Deb Dana** — *The Polyvagal Theory in Therapy* (2018). Practical application of Porges's work. The "ladder" metaphor for autonomic states is useful for understanding participant behavior during sessions.
- **Bessel van der Kolk** — *The Body Keeps the Score* (2014). The relationship between trauma, embodiment, and expression. Relevant to understanding why organizations — not just individuals — carry unprocessed experience.

## Relational Ontology & Assemblage Theory

The philosophical framework behind the Bilingual Dashboard and the analysis of systemic alienation from honest expression.

- **Gilles Deleuze & Félix Guattari** — *A Thousand Plateaus* (1987). The Royal/Nomadic distinction originates here. The concepts of assemblage, deterritorialization, and smooth vs. striated space inform how RP understands organizational dynamics.
- **Manuel DeLanda** — *A New Philosophy of Society* (2006). Assemblage theory made accessible. DeLanda's distinction between properties and capacities is useful for understanding what organizations can do vs. what they actually do.
- **Karen Barad** — *Meeting the Universe Halfway* (2007). Agential realism and the concept of intra-action. Relevant to understanding how measurement participates in creating what it measures (directly applicable to the Bilingual Dashboard).

## Narrative & Expression

The craft layer — how stories work and why honest expression matters.

- **Robert McKee** — *Story* (1997). Narrative structure. Useful for understanding the arc of a Mirror Narrative and how to structure film edits.
- **Brené Brown** — *Daring Greatly* (2012). Vulnerability as a professional asset. Relevant to helping clients understand why saying the scary thing is the strategic thing.
- **Marshall Rosenberg** — *Nonviolent Communication* (2003). The distinction between observation and evaluation informs facilitation technique. Useful for maintaining the "observe and reflect, don't diagnose" principle.

## Documentary & Participatory Media

The production methodology lineage.

- **Jean Rouch** — Cinéma vérité pioneer. The idea that the camera's presence is part of the truth, not an obstacle to it.
- **Trinh T. Minh-ha** — *Reassemblage* (1982). Anti-ethnographic filmmaking. The ethics of representation and the power dynamics of who holds the camera.
- **Insight Media / Participatory Video** — Various practitioners. The tradition of putting cameras in community members' hands. RP's camera-shipping model is a direct descendant of participatory video methodology.

## Measurement & Evaluation

The Bilingual Dashboard's intellectual context.

- **Michael Quinn Patton** — *Developmental Evaluation* (2010). Evaluation as a real-time, adaptive practice rather than a retrospective judgment. The Bilingual Dashboard's Nomadic register is directly influenced by Patton's developmental evaluation framework.
- **Margaret Wheatley** — *Leadership and the New Science* (2006). Systems thinking applied to organizations. The concept that measurement changes the system being measured.

## Human Design & Energetic Practice

The Generator energy principles that govern capacity and decision-making.

- **Ra Uru Hu** — *The Definitive Book of Human Design* (2011). The system that informs Isaac's Sacral authority, Generator strategy, and capacity management principles.
- **Richard Rudd** — *The Gene Keys* (2009). The shadow-to-gift framework used in Isaac's inner practice. Relevant background for understanding why personal integration is not separate from professional methodology.

---

## How to Use This List

This is a reference, not a curriculum. The most immediately useful sources for understanding the RP methodology:

1. **Porges** (for facilitation science)
2. **Deleuze & Guattari** (for the Royal/Nomadic framework)
3. **Patton** (for the measurement philosophy)
4. **McKee** (for narrative craft)

Everything else deepens the practice but isn't prerequisite to doing the work.
