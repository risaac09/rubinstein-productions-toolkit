# Methodology Deep-Dive: The Four Phases

The RP methodology moves through four phases. The pace varies by engagement; the sequence does not.

## Phase 1: Container

**Purpose:** Create safety. Establish the relational field.

The Container phase is about making the environment safe enough for honesty. This is not a metaphor — it is grounded in polyvagal theory. When the nervous system detects safety (ventral vagal activation), narrative coherence naturally increases. When it detects threat, people default to rehearsed, defensive language.

### What Happens

- Environmental setup: space, light, temperature, privacy
- Relational warm-up: conversational, non-performative
- Consent loops: ongoing, revisitable, embodied (not just a signature)
- Internal regulation: facilitator enters a ventral-vagal state before the participant arrives

### Key Principles

**Facilitation before technology.** The relational container is established before any camera is set up, any recording is started, any formal process begins.

**Your body is part of the set.** The facilitator's regulated nervous system entrains the participant's state through facial cues, vocal tone, and pacing. This is not woo — it's documented co-regulation science (Porges, 2011).

**Narrate the technical.** When setting up equipment, explain what you're doing and why. "I'm adjusting this light — let me know if it's too bright." This reduces surveillance anxiety.

### Common Mistakes

- Rushing to content because the session feels "short"
- Treating consent as a one-time event rather than an ongoing loop
- Bringing your own nervous-system dysregulation into the space
- Over-explaining the methodology (the participant doesn't need to understand polyvagal theory)

## Phase 2: Excavation

**Purpose:** Listen for what's alive. Find the inside version of the story.

Excavation is the research phase — but "research" here means something different from academic extraction. The goal is to surface what the organization or individual already knows but hasn't said. The facilitator's job is to create conditions for that truth to emerge, not to extract it.

### What Happens

- Deep interviews with stakeholders closest to the work
- Observation of organizational dynamics (meetings, spaces, informal interactions)
- Listening for: what language is natural vs. borrowed, where energy rises vs. drops, what's said easily vs. with effort
- Finding the gap between the official narrative and the felt reality

### Key Principles

**Listen for the gap.** Every organization has two stories: the one they tell publicly and the one they live. The gap between them is where the work lives.

**Follow the energy.** When someone lights up talking about something, lean in. When they go flat, notice what just happened.

**Don't diagnose.** The facilitator observes and reflects. Diagnosis implies authority over the client's experience, which undermines the entire methodology.

### The Inside Version

The most important output of Excavation is what we call "the inside version" — the narrative that lives beneath the official one. This isn't hidden in the sense of being secret; it's hidden in the sense that no one has named it yet. The Mirror Narrative is the written form of this inside version.

## Phase 3: Performance

**Purpose:** Capture truth on participants' own terms.

Performance is the production phase, but "performance" here is deliberately reframed. Participants are not performing for the camera — they are performing their own authorship. The camera ships to them. They film themselves, in their own space, on their own schedule.

### What Happens

- Camera kit ships to participant (see [Equipment & Logistics](../operations/equipment-logistics.md))
- Orientation call: 15 minutes, how to use the kit, what to film
- Filming window: typically 1–2 weeks
- Facilitator available by text for questions
- Kit returned, footage ingested and backed up

### Key Principles

**The camera is a witness, not a product.** It creates a space for reflection, not a marketing asset. The quality of the footage matters less than the honesty of the expression.

**Participant as author.** They decide what to film, when, where, how long. They can delete anything before returning the kit. The power stays with them.

**Sound over image.** Audiences forgive visual imperfection but not bad audio. The lavalier microphone is the most important piece of equipment in the kit.

### What Makes This Different

Most documentary work follows an extractive model: crew arrives, shoots, leaves, edits, publishes. The participant has limited authorship. RP inverts this: the camera goes to the participant, they author their own footage, everything is returned. The facilitator's role is editorial (Translation phase), not directorial.

## Phase 4: Translation

**Purpose:** Edit. Return. The story travels without the facilitator.

Translation is the final phase — turning raw material into forms that travel. This includes editing footage, writing reports, assembling deliverables, and transferring everything to the client.

### What Happens

- Footage review and logging
- Rough cut editing (DaVinci Resolve)
- Client review cycle: rough cut → feedback → revision → final
- Bilingual Dashboard Report writing (both Royal and Nomadic registers)
- Final delivery: all assets, footage, transcripts, reports transferred to client
- All RP copies deleted after 90-day confirmation window

### Key Principles

**5-day latency.** Never edit within 5 days of filming or receiving footage. The subconscious processes material during the gap, producing better editorial decisions.

**Client review is collaborative, not performative.** Share rough cuts early. Invite honest feedback. Don't present a "final" product and ask for approval — that recreates the institutional dynamic the methodology is designed to break.

**The story must travel without you.** The final product should be usable without the facilitator in the room. If it requires explanation, it's not done.

**Return everything.** All footage, all data, all artifacts belong to the client. RP retains nothing. This is not just ethical — it's methodological. Returning materials completes the power transfer and eliminates extractive dynamics.

---

## Phase Interactions

The phases are sequential but not rigid. In practice:

- Container is established once but **maintained** throughout
- Excavation discoveries may **reshape** what gets filmed in Performance
- Translation insights may **reveal** things missed in Excavation
- The Bilingual Dashboard operates across all four phases, not just Translation

The methodology is a helix, not a waterfall. You move through the phases once, but each phase informs the others retroactively.
