Rubinstein Productions

Four phases. One principle.

Every engagement moves through this arc. The pace varies; the sequence doesn't.
1
Container
Create safety. Establish the relational field. The facilitator's regulated nervous system entrains the participant's state through co-regulation.
2
Excavation
Listen for what's alive. Find the inside version of the story — the one underneath the official narrative. Observe, don't diagnose.
3
Performance
Cameras ship to participants. They film on their own terms, in their own space. The camera is a witness, not a product. Truth gets captured, not performed.
4
Translation
Edit. Return. The story travels without the facilitator. All materials — footage, data, artifacts — belong to the client entirely.
The methodology is a helix, not a waterfall. Each phase informs the others retroactively.

Core Principles

Facilitation Before Technology
The relational container matters more than the equipment. A well-facilitated iPhone interview outperforms a poorly facilitated cinema shoot.
Reciprocity Over Extraction
Every interaction is a mutual exchange. The participant should feel they received something — not that something was taken.
Safety as Production Asset
Emotional regulation directly produces narrative coherence. This is a technical principle, not a soft one.
Visible Consent Loops
Consent is ongoing, revisitable, and embodied. Check in at minimum three times per session — not just once with a signature.
Embodied Presence
The facilitator's regulated nervous system is part of the production environment. Your body stabilizes the participant's through co-regulation.
The Story Travels Without You
Success is when the client doesn't need the facilitator. Build capacity, not dependency. Return everything.
