# Practices

Two practices, one operator. The practices share back-of-house operations (financial, capacity, IT, BD pipeline tooling) but enforce hard separation in client-facing voice, website, and methodology naming.

|---|---|---|---|

| **[Independent Evaluation](evaluation.md)** | isaacrubinstein.com | Plain MPH-credentialed | `00 Canonical/Independent Evaluator - Positioning.md` |

## Why two

The methodologies are siblings — both grounded in participatory, stakeholder-centered work and the Bilingual Dashboard logic. The audiences are different. A nonprofit ED scanning eval RFPs needs methodological rigor and credentialing. A founder considering Say Why needs evidence the practice will hold their weight, not summarize them into a deck.

Drift between the two registers is the single highest reputation risk to either practice. The voice firewall is non-negotiable.

## When work crosses

Some engagements touch both: a Say Why client requests an evaluation deliverable; an evaluation client benefits from facilitation. The defaults:

- **Default no.** Cross-practice work doubles brand-management overhead.
- **Exception:** if both sides see clear value AND the deliverable can be cleanly attributed to one practice's voice, route through that practice's pricing and language.
- **Never:** mix voices in a single deliverable. The Bilingual Dashboard is the only artifact that cleanly bridges, and even there the framing differs by audience.
