## SHOOT 1: Camera Packing (30 min)

This is the most ownable visual in the entire brand. Nobody else does this.

### Setup
- Natural window light. Table or desk surface.
- Camera body + lens laid out. A box or padded case. Shipping label if you have one.
- iPhone filming from above (bird's eye) or from across the table at a slight angle.
- No ring light. No tripod necessary — prop the phone against something.

### What to film
1. **Hands on the camera.** Pick it up. Turn it over. Hold the lens. 10 seconds.
2. **Wrapping.** Wrap the camera in a cloth or padding. Place it in the box. 15 seconds.
3. **The lens.** Same thing — hold it, place it in. 10 seconds.
4. **Closing the box.** Tape it or close the lid. 5 seconds.
5. **The label.** If you have a shipping label, write on it or place it on the box. If not, skip this.
6. **Final shot.** The sealed box sitting on the table. Hold for 5 seconds.

### Technical notes
- Film in 4K if your iPhone supports it (Settings → Camera → Record Video → 4K 30fps)
- Shoot each action as a separate clip. Easier to edit.
- No talking in this one. Hands and objects only.
- Silence or ambient room sound. No music.
- Total raw footage: ~2-3 minutes. Will cut to 30-60 seconds.

### What this becomes
- LinkedIn video post (with text overlay: "I send you a camera.")
- Website hero background (if we want movement later)
- Instagram story / reel
- The single most compelling visual proof that this offering is real

## SHOOT 2: Yapper Video (30 min)

This becomes Thursday's LinkedIn post. Film it now, post it Thursday.

### Setup
- iPhone propped at eye level (stack books, use a shelf)
- Natural light — face a window. No overhead lighting.
- Quiet room. No echo if possible.
- Frame: head and shoulders, some space above. Slightly off-center is fine.
- Wear something neutral. No logos.

### Talking points (NOT a script — say it in your own words)

The through-line: **Why the most qualified people give the flattest pitches.**

Points to hit:
1. The consultants who struggle most to describe what they do are usually the most qualified.
2. That's not a coincidence. The systems that build expertise — academia, nonprofits, corporate — train you to hedge, caveat, and optimize for not being wrong.
3. Those are survival skills inside the institution. Outside it, they make you invisible.
4. The honest version of your pitch usually only comes out after two drinks at a conference. Or in a private conversation with someone you trust.
5. That's the version worth building from.
6. (Optional close — only if it feels natural:) I send people a camera and have them film that version.

### Technical notes
- 60-90 seconds max. If it goes longer, that's fine — you'll trim.
- Do 3 takes. The second or third is usually the best.
- Minor jump cuts are fine and expected in this format.
- Look at the camera lens, not the screen.
- Start talking immediately — no "hey guys" or throat clearing.
- End clean. Just stop. Don't sign off.

### What this becomes
- Thursday's LinkedIn post (Post 3 from the weekly content plan)
- Can be repurposed for website
- Sets the visual tone for all future RP video content
