# Nomadic Indicators Codebook
## Qualitative Coding Guide for Emergence & Systemic Change

*For Emergent Narrative Impact Framework Register 2: Nomadic Science*

---

## Purpose

This codebook provides a systematic approach to analyzing qualitative data from client engagements—session transcripts, recordings, field notes, and follow-up conversations. Unlike Royal Metrics (which track quantifiable outcomes), Nomadic Indicators track what emerges, shifts, and transforms through the work.

> "The Nomadic Scientist is not trying to prove a hypothesis but to follow the emergence of something genuinely new." — From the Framework

---

## Core Coding Domains

### Domain 1: Consistency Mapping

**Definition:** Consistency is not coherence (logical alignment) but how heterogeneous elements hang together *despite* contradictions. High consistency means the narrative holds complexity without flattening it.

#### Code: CONSISTENCY-HIGH
**Apply when:** Stakeholder articulates something that holds multiple truths simultaneously without resolving the tension artificially.

**Example:**
> "We're a startup that moves fast AND we care deeply about our team's wellbeing—those aren't contradictions, they're both true, and sometimes that creates friction we have to navigate."

**Markers:**
- Both/and language instead of either/or
- Acknowledgment of paradox without anxiety
- Ability to hold stakeholder differences without needing agreement

#### Code: CONSISTENCY-LOW
**Apply when:** Narrative collapses complexity into oversimplified resolution or fractures into incoherent pieces.

**Example:**
> "Well, we used to care about quality, but now we just focus on growth." (False dichotomy)

**Markers:**
- Either/or framing
- Denial of complexity
- Different stakeholders telling incompatible stories

#### Code: CONSISTENCY-SHIFT
**Apply when:** Movement from low to high consistency is observable within or across sessions.

**Example:**
> Session 1: "I can't talk about the spiritual dimension—funders won't get it."
> Session 3: "The work IS spiritual—and I'm learning to translate that for different audiences without losing it."

---

### Domain 2: Conversational Texture

**Definition:** The qualitative feel of how someone speaks—not just what they say but how they say it. Changes in texture often signal transformation.

#### Code: PAUSE-CONTEMPLATIVE
**Apply when:** Silence indicates processing, integration, or genuine reflection (not confusion or avoidance).

**Markers:**
- Pause followed by deeper insight
- Client says "let me think about that" and genuinely does
- Breath shifts before speaking

#### Code: PAUSE-AVOIDANT
**Apply when:** Silence indicates deflection, discomfort, or shutdown.

**Markers:**
- Topic change after pause
- Physical withdrawal (looking away, crossing arms)
- "I don't know" said quickly to end inquiry

#### Code: SELF-CORRECTION
**Apply when:** Client catches themselves mid-thought and revises toward greater truth.

**Example:**
> "We want to scale— actually, wait. Do we want to scale? I've been saying that but I'm not sure it's true."

**Significance:** High self-correction frequency indicates active integration happening in real-time.

#### Code: METAPHOR-EMERGENCE
**Apply when:** Client spontaneously generates a metaphor to describe their work or situation.

**Example:**
> "It's like we've been building a house on a foundation we never examined."

**Significance:** Metaphors reveal unconscious pattern recognition. Track which metaphors recur and evolve.

#### Code: PRONOUN-SHIFT
**Apply when:** Movement between "I/me" and "we/us" or "they/them" indicates relational or ownership changes.

**Example:**
> Session 1: "They need to understand what we do."
> Session 4: "We need to help them see themselves in our story."

**Track:** Direction of shift and what it signals about responsibility, inclusion, or distancing.

---

### Domain 3: Rhizomatic Process

**Definition:** Tracking how rigid organizational thinking breaks open (deterritorialization) and how new structures form around emergent insights (reterritorialization).

#### Code: DETERRITORIALIZATION
**Apply when:** A fixed belief, assumption, or structure breaks open.

**Example:**
> "I always thought we had to sound 'professional' to be taken seriously. But that's just fear dressed up as strategy."

**Markers:**
- "I never thought about it that way"
- Questioning long-held assumptions
- Visible disorientation followed by relief
- Laughter of recognition

#### Code: RETERRITORIALIZATION-GENERATIVE
**Apply when:** New structure forms that's more alive, coherent, or resonant than what it replaced.

**Example:**
> "So instead of 'consulting firm,' we're a 'thinking partner for organizations in transition.' That actually feels like us."

**Markers:**
- Energy increase when articulating
- Statement passes the "body test" (feels true somatically)
- Creates new possibilities rather than closing them down

#### Code: RETERRITORIALIZATION-RIGID
**Apply when:** New structure forms but recreates old patterns or prematurely closes possibility.

**Example:**
> "Okay, so our new tagline is [X]." (Said with urgency to resolve discomfort rather than genuine arrival)

**Markers:**
- Energy decrease or flatness
- Feels like homework completed rather than truth discovered
- Doesn't survive contact with different stakeholders

#### Code: LINE-OF-FLIGHT
**Apply when:** An insight emerges that wasn't in the original scope—something genuinely new that opens unexpected territory.

**Example:**
> "Wait—what if our real work isn't the program at all? What if it's the community that forms around it?"

**Significance:** Lines of flight are the gold of Nomadic science. They indicate the work is catalyzing genuine emergence, not just clarifying what was already known.

---

### Domain 4: Fear Inventory

**Definition:** Tracking the structure, transformation, and dissolution of fear throughout the engagement.

#### Code: FEAR-SURFACE
**Apply when:** Client articulates a presenting fear—the first layer of what they're afraid of.

**Example:**
> "I'm afraid funders won't take us seriously."

#### Code: FEAR-UNDERLYING
**Apply when:** Deeper fear beneath the surface fear is identified.

**Example:**
> "Actually, I'm afraid of being seen as unprofessional—like I don't belong in these rooms."

#### Code: FEAR-ROOT
**Apply when:** The core fear is reached—usually related to identity, worth, or belonging.

**Example:**
> "I'm afraid that if people see the real me, they'll realize I don't know what I'm doing."

#### Code: FEAR-TRANSFORMATION
**Apply when:** The relationship to fear shifts—not that fear disappears, but it's held differently.

**Example:**
> "I still feel the fear, but now I can name it and speak anyway. It doesn't run the show anymore."

**Track:** The journey from Surface → Underlying → Root → Transformation for each client.

---

### Domain 5: Somatic Markers

**Definition:** Observable body-based signals that indicate nervous system state and truth-resonance.

#### Code: SOMATIC-OPENING
**Apply when:** Body signals increased safety, presence, or resonance.

**Markers:**
- Deeper breathing
- Shoulders dropping
- Eye contact increasing
- Leaning forward
- Voice dropping in pitch
- Face softening

#### Code: SOMATIC-CLOSING
**Apply when:** Body signals defensiveness, disconnection, or protection.

**Markers:**
- Shallow breathing
- Shoulders rising
- Eye contact decreasing
- Leaning back or crossing arms
- Voice tightening
- Facial tension

#### Code: SOMATIC-BREAKTHROUGH
**Apply when:** Visible release or shift in body accompanying insight.

**Markers:**
- Tears (not necessarily sad—often recognition or relief)
- Deep exhale
- Laughter of recognition
- Physical movement/gesture
- "I can feel that in my body"

---

### Domain 6: Relational Field

**Definition:** Changes in how the client relates to stakeholders, audience, or you (the facilitator).

#### Code: RELATION-EXPANSION
**Apply when:** Client's sense of who they're speaking to/for expands.

**Example:**
> "I was thinking about donors, but really, we need to speak to the community members first."

#### Code: RELATION-CONTRACTION
**Apply when:** Client narrows their audience out of fear or protection.

**Example:**
> "Maybe we should just focus on people who already get it."

#### Code: PROJECTION-IDENTIFIED
**Apply when:** Client recognizes they've been projecting assumptions onto an audience.

**Example:**
> "I've been assuming funders only care about numbers. But I've never actually asked them."

#### Code: FACILITATOR-TRANSFERENCE
**Apply when:** Client's relationship to you (the facilitator) reveals something about their patterns.

**Example:**
> Client seeking excessive approval → Pattern of external validation dependency
> Client resisting all suggestions → Pattern of defending against input

**Note:** Use with care. Track for your own awareness and supervision, not necessarily for client-facing discussion.

## Coding Process

### Step 1: Initial Read/Listen
- Go through transcript or recording without coding
- Note overall impression and energy arc
- Mark moments that feel significant

### Step 2: First Pass Coding
- Apply codes from all six domains
- Be generous—over-code rather than under-code
- Note timestamps or paragraph references

### Step 3: Pattern Identification
- Look for clustering of codes
- Identify journey arcs (e.g., fear evolution)
- Note surprises or contradictions

### Step 4: Synthesis
- Write 1-2 paragraph narrative summary
- Identify 2-3 key insights
- Note questions for next session or follow-up

### Step 5: Integration
- Transfer relevant findings to case study template
- Update Royal Metrics Dashboard if quantifiable outcomes emerged
- Note methodology learnings

## Consistency Scoring Protocol

*For use in follow-up assessments*

### The Three Consistency Questions

Have diverse stakeholders (staff, board, beneficiaries) rate 1-10:

1. **"This narrative holds my contradictory experience."**
   - Measures: Ability to contain complexity

2. **"I can locate myself in this story without erasing complexity."**
   - Measures: Inclusive resonance

3. **"This makes our conflicts generative rather than destructive."**
   - Measures: Functional transformation of tension

### Scoring

**Average Score Interpretation:**
- 9-10: Exceptional consistency—narrative functions as living container
- 7-8: Strong consistency—holds most complexity effectively
- 5-6: Moderate consistency—works for some stakeholders, not others
- 3-4: Low consistency—significant gaps or fractures
- 1-2: Incoherent—narrative not functioning as unifier

### Cross-Stakeholder Analysis

**Variance Analysis:** High variance between groups indicates consistency failure at intersections.

## Sample Coded Passage

**Raw Transcript:**
> "I guess we're... [5 second pause]... you know, I've been saying we're a 'capacity building organization' but that feels so dead when I say it. [laughs] Like, nobody gets excited about capacity building. What we actually do is... we help communities remember what they already know. That sounds weird but it's true. But I can't say that to a funder, can I?"

**Codes Applied:**
- PAUSE-CONTEMPLATIVE (5 second pause before insight)
- DETERRITORIALIZATION (questioning "capacity building" frame)
- METAPHOR-EMERGENCE ("help communities remember what they already know")
- SOMATIC-OPENING (laughter of recognition)
- FEAR-SURFACE ("can't say that to a funder")
- LINE-OF-FLIGHT (new frame emerging)

**Significance Note:**
This is a key moment. The client is spontaneously generating more resonant language but immediately qualifying it with fear. Follow-up question: "What happens if you imagine saying exactly that to a funder?"

## Monthly Synthesis Ritual Integration

During your monthly synthesis ritual, review coded materials across all clients to identify:

1. **Cross-Client Patterns:** Are certain codes appearing across multiple engagements?
2. **Methodology Signals:** Which facilitation approaches generate more Lines of Flight?
3. **Personal Patterns:** Where are you consistently seeing Deterritorialization? Resistance?
4. **Theoretical Insights:** What's this teaching you about narrative transformation?
