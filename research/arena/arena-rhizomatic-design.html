Arborescent vs. Rhizomatic — A System for Conceptual Data

The Arena has been a tree. Claims entered domains, fought neighbors, climbed brackets, got culled by tier. The hierarchy is real — but it's path-dependent. What if the structure made the winners?

The rhizomatic arm doesn't rank. It maps. Every claim meets every other claim. No brackets, no clusters, no elimination until all encounters are complete. The data isn't "who's on top" — it's the network of absorptions, resistances, and transformations across all 171 possible connections.

What Is Conceptual Data?

Not empirical measurement. Not opinion. Conceptual data is the structured residue of adversarial theoretical encounter — what's left after claims have been forced to fight, absorb, merge, and die under consistent rules.

Three forms:

Pattern Data

Which theoretical moves consistently survive stress-testing across different opponents and formats. "Recursive meta-critique beats domain-specific mechanism claims" is a finding with N>1. The pattern is the data.

win rate per opponent type kill criteria hit frequency absorption success rate
Vulnerability Maps

Each claim's documented susceptibility profile across all encounters. Comparable the way side-effect profiles are compared across interventions. A claim failing on Therapeutic Reductionism in 3/4 bouts has a reliability problem.

criteria × claim matrix conditional failure rate opponent-dependent triggers
Absorption Genealogies

Directed graph of which claims subsume which others, with edge conditions. The topology itself is data — it reveals conceptual geography no single claim states. Convergent absorption patterns across both arms = high-confidence finding.

directed absorption graph cluster coefficient bridge nodes

The Comparison Layer — What Convergence Tells You

Convergent findings are where both arms agree — same claims rise, same absorptions hold, same vulnerabilities surface regardless of structure. These are the Arena's highest-confidence outputs.

Divergent findings reveal bracket artifacts — claims that won because of who they fought, not what they are. Equally valuable: they tell you where your certainty should be lower.

Emergent topology is what only the rhizome can see: bridge nodes (claims that connect otherwise separate clusters), hub claims (absorb many, resist many), and isolates (claims that neither absorb nor get absorbed — genuinely orthogonal). The tree can't produce this because it pre-sorts claims into clusters before they fight.

Rhizomatic Bout Design — 171 Encounters

Every claim fights every other claim exactly once. Randomized encounter order. Same 5-move format, same 16 kill criteria, same scoring rubric. But no elimination — every claim completes all 18 of its bouts before any verdict is rendered.

Post-bout analysis generates per-claim:

Connection Score

Not a rank — a density measure. How many claims can this one productively engage with (neither absorbing nor being absorbed)? High connection = conceptual bridge. Low connection = specialized tool.

Absorption Asymmetry

Ratio of "I absorb you" to "you absorb me" across all opponents. ABKT's meta-critical power predicts high asymmetry. But does it hold against practice-level claims it never faced in Arm A?

Kill Criteria Signature

Heat map of which criteria hit this claim across all 18 opponents. Each claim gets a unique vulnerability fingerprint. Compare fingerprints across arms for robustness.

The question isn't "which claim is best." The question is: what does the conceptual field look like when you let every idea touch every other idea without pre-sorting them into domains? The tree gives you a winner. The rhizome gives you a map. You need both — the tree to decide, the rhizome to understand.

Defining the Term: Conceptual Data

Conceptual data is the structured, comparable, reproducible residue of systematic adversarial encounter between theoretical claims — evaluated under consistent criteria, across multiple matchup structures, with documented vulnerability profiles and absorption genealogies.

It is not empirical data (no measurement of phenomena). It is not opinion (consistent rules, documented outcomes). It is not meta-analysis in the Cochrane sense (no effect sizes). It is closest to:

A systematic theoretical review with internal validity checks — where the "studies" being reviewed are conceptual claims, the "methodology" is adversarial stress-testing, and the "data" is the pattern of outcomes across all encounters.

Properties of conceptual data:

Structured — generated under consistent rules (kill criteria, scoring rubric, bout format)
Comparable — same metrics across all claims (vulnerability profiles, absorption rates)
Reproducible — re-runnable under different structures (the sensitivity analysis)
Falsifiable — a claim predicted to be robust can be shown to be bracket-dependent
Topological — the shape of the network is itself a finding
Non-empirical — tests coherence and robustness, not truth-in-the-world

The Arena doesn't tell you what's true. It tells you what survives. The difference between those two things is the space where the next research program lives.
