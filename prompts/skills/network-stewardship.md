# Network Stewardship
**The relational field — living, not logged**
*Version: 1.0 | March 2026*

---

## PHILOSOPHY

A Generator's network is not a list. It is a field of mutual resonance — people who energize, who challenge, who open doors Isaac didn't know existed, who receive what he offers and offer what he receives.

The COO skill manages clients and pipeline. This skill manages *relationships* — the broader web of people in Isaac's world who may or may not be RP clients, but who matter.

**Operating principle:** Give before you need. Show up before you want something. The Generator's relational power is in *response* — being genuinely present when someone reaches out, and reaching out only when it feels true.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TIERS

### Tier 1 — Core Circle (5–10 people)
Relationships of deep mutual trust, genuine intimacy, frequent contact.
- Who: closest collaborators, partners, mentors, friends who *know* Isaac
- Cadence: ongoing, no structure needed — these are alive
- Stewardship: show up fully when present; protect time for depth

### Tier 2 — Strategic Allies (20–30 people)
Relationships of aligned mission, mutual respect, and periodic collaboration or referral.
- Who: colleagues in adjacent fields, past clients who became partners, people who consistently refer, mentors and peers who matter professionally
- Cadence: quarterly minimum touch-point; ideally monthly awareness
- Stewardship: personalized outreach, genuine interest in their work, offer before asking

### Tier 3 — Extended Field (50–200 people)
People in Isaac's broader ecosystem — LinkedIn connections who engage meaningfully, contacts from events, former colleagues, interesting strangers who became light acquaintances.
- Who: anyone Isaac has a meaningful memory of an interaction with
- Cadence: 1–2x/year or when something specific creates a genuine reason
- Stewardship: notice when they publish something relevant; congratulate milestones; share things that made you think of them specifically

## WEEKLY RELATIONSHIP SCAN

Run during weekly review:

1. **Who came to mind this week** — not to pitch, just genuinely thought of? That's a signal. Reach out.
2. **Any Tier 2 relationship going cold** — not contacted in 90+ days? Schedule a touch-point.
3. **Who helped me this week** — explicitly or implicitly? Express gratitude (this week, not eventually).
4. **Any new connection worth nurturing** — met someone at an event, had a meaningful conversation? Move them to appropriate tier.
5. **Who's going through something** — a transition, a challenge, a win? That's when relationships deepen.

---

## OUTREACH DECISION PROTOCOL

Before reaching out to anyone for *relational* reasons (not RP pipeline):

1. **Why now?** — What's the genuine reason? (Something they published, a memory, a win they'd want to celebrate, something you want to share)
2. **What's the offer?** — Can you bring something to this? Or is this pure extraction?
3. **What's the CTA?** — Coffee, phone call, no CTA at all (just a note), forward something useful
4. **Sacral check:** Does this feel genuine or performative?

If performative → don't send. Relationships sense this.

**Route to `outreach-email-manager`** when the touch-point needs a drafted email. This skill does the *thinking*; outreach-email-manager does the *writing*.

## GRATITUDE PRACTICE (WEEKLY)

Every week: identify 1–3 people to thank for something specific. Not "thanks for everything you do" — specific. "Your question about [X] in our last conversation changed how I'm thinking about [Y]."

Gratitude is not transactional. It does not require a response. It is a practice of noticing what's been given.

---

## COLLABORATIVE OPPORTUNITY SCANNING

During quarterly review, scan Tier 2 for:
- Who is doing work that overlaps with where RP is going?
- Who has an audience that would benefit from Voice Liberation?
- Who am I in a position to refer or amplify right now?
- Any conversations that have been circling without landing? Name the collaboration explicitly.

**Generator principle:** Don't initiate collaborations speculatively. Wait for the moment of natural overlap — then respond to it definitively.

---

## RELATIONSHIP FIELD HEALTH INDICATORS

**Healthy field:**
- Giving and receiving feel roughly balanced across the system
- Relationships feel alive, not maintained
- Isaac is being referred to people he'd enjoy working with
- Occasional unexpected reconnections (field is active)
- Gratitude comes naturally, not as a strategy

**Depleted field:**
- Reaching out only when needing something
- Long gaps in Tier 2 contact
- Feeling isolated or like "no one knows what I'm doing"
- Referrals have dried up
- Resentment about not being supported (often a sign of under-giving)

When field feels depleted: **give first**. Reach out to 3 people this week with no ask. Share something useful. Express gratitude. The field responds.

## WHAT BELONGS IN OBSIDIAN

Maintain a running relationship log in `Obsidian/Personal/Network/`:

```
## [Name] | [Tier] | [Last contact: date]
- Context: [how you know them, why they matter]
- Recent: [what's happening in their world]
- Last interaction: [what was discussed]
- Next: [anything to follow up on]
```

When a meaningful conversation happens, log a brief note. Not everything — just what matters. The goal is to remember what's real, not to manage people.

---

## INTEGRATION WITH RP PIPELINE

Some Tier 2 and 3 relationships may become RP clients or referral sources. This is natural, not manipulative — the work should speak for itself.

**The line:** Never treat a relationship as a lead. If someone in your network is a potential fit for RP, let the relationship develop and *wait for the invitation* — or offer once, clearly, when the moment is right. Never pressure. Never follow up on a "sales" ask to someone who is also a friend.

When a relational outreach becomes RP relevant → route to `rubinstein-productions-agent` for the business layer. Keep the relationship layer clean.
