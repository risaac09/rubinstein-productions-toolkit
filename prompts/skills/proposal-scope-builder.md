# Proposal & Scope Builder

## Overview

This skill helps Isaac create professional, mission-aligned proposals that convert inquiries into booked Rubinstein Productions engagements while protecting his pricing boundaries and energy.

---

## When to Use This Skill

**Use when:**
- Client has expressed interest and needs a formal proposal
- Responding to RFPs or project inquiries
- Pricing a project and need to ensure it meets minimums
- Converting warm outreach responses into concrete offers
- Creating retainer or ongoing engagement proposals

**Do NOT use for:**
- Initial outreach (use `outreach-email-manager` skill)
- General pricing questions (consult `rubinstein-productions-coo` skill)
- Contract creation (this creates proposals only)

## Workflow: Creating a Proposal

### Step 1: Discovery Conversation

Before generating any proposal, conduct a brief discovery:

**Essential Questions:**
1. "Tell me about your organization and what's bringing you here"
2. "What's the gap between how you describe your work internally and how you're currently telling it publicly?"
3. "Who's your audience, and what do you want them to *feel* — not just understand?"
4. "What's your timeline?"
5. "Do you have a budget range in mind?"
6. "Is your narrative already clear, or is the excavation part of what you need?"

**Information to Gather:**
- Organization mission and current narrative state
- Target audience and desired impact
- Timeline/urgency
- Budget range
- Narrative clarity (clear → delivery focus; unclear → excavation needed)
- Decision-maker relationship to the work (personal investment vs. delegating)

**Red Flags (Decline Before Investing Time):**
- Budget below $500 floor
- "We need this cheap and fast"
- "Just make it look good" (transactional, not transformational)
- Values misalignment — purely promotional, no interest in truth-telling
- Isaac at capacity (check COO skill)

---

### Step 2: Select Appropriate Service Tier

| Client Situation | Recommended Tier |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| Wants 1:1 narrative clarity work | Tier 1 — Founder Story (Narrative Clarity Sessions) |
| Organization needs strategy sprint | Tier 2 — Program Engagement (Strategic Facilitation Sprint) |
| Ongoing partnership / stewardship | Tier 3 — Organizational Embedding (Strategic Partnership) |
| Unclear message + needs full production | Tier 2 → Tier 3 pathway |

**Selection Logic:**
- Individual founder or leader, personal narrative work → Founder Story
- Team or organization, needs structured output (Dashboard, report) → Program Engagement
- Recurring engagement, ongoing strategy → Organizational Embedding
- When in doubt: start with Program Engagement, offer Organizational Embedding as natural next step in proposal

---

### Step 3: Pricing (Always Check COO Guardrails First)

**Tier 1 — Founder Story (Narrative Clarity Sessions)**
- Single session: $1,500–$2,500

**Tier 2 — Program Engagement (Strategic Facilitation Sprint)**
- Half-day: $3,000–$5,000
- Full sprint (3–5 days): $3,000–$8,000

**Tier 3 — Organizational Embedding (Strategic Partnership)**
- Monthly retainer: $4,000–$8,000/month
- Minimum 3-month commitment

**Floor:** $500/hr. Never go below regardless of framing.

**Pricing principles:**
- Anchor to high end first — negotiate down, never up
- Discounts max 30% for aligned nonprofits; 20% maximum otherwise
- Add-ons: Bilingual Dashboard formatting, extra facilitation days, AI production workflow delivery
- Rush delivery (< 2 weeks): +20%

**Gut check after calculating:** Does this feel sustainable for Isaac's energy given what's being asked? If not, scope is wrong — don't underprice, rescope.

---

### Step 4: Generate the Proposal

**Proposal structure:**

1. **Opening** — Connect to their mission in 2-3 sentences that show you've listened. Not generic. Reference the specific thing they said that revealed the gap.

2. **Understanding Your Situation** — Name the pattern of alienation from honest expression you're seeing. "Here's what I'm hearing between the lines..." This is where RP demonstrates depth.

3. **Proposed Engagement** — Service tier, specific deliverables, timeline with phases
   - Be specific about what they'll receive (Bilingual Dashboard, facilitation sessions, asset formats)
   - Name the Zero-Measurement Zone (if filming involved) and what that means for them

4. **Investment** — Clear breakdown; payment terms (typically 50% deposit, 50% on delivery or monthly for Territory)

5. **Why This Approach** — Brief articulation of the facilitation and film methodology. Why this approach, not just content strategy.

6. **Next Steps** — One clear action to move forward

**Tone:** Warm and precise. Confident without being salesy. The proposal itself should demonstrate the methodology — it should feel like the work has already begun.

---

### Step 5: Deliver + Follow-Up Strategy

1. Isaac reviews → Sacral check on pricing ("Does this feel clean?")
2. Send to client
3. Follow-up at 3, 7, 14 days if no response
4. Archive after 14 days of silence — don't chase

**Follow-up language:**
- Day 3: "Wanted to make sure this reached you. Any questions?"
- Day 7: "I have availability opening up — wanted to see if this timing works for you."
- Day 14: "Checking in one last time. Happy to adjust scope if timing isn't right."

---

## Decision Support: Should Isaac Take This?

Run the Client Acceptance Filter:

1. Sacral yes? (gut, not cognitive override)
2. Capacity available? (≤2 active Organizational Embedding, ≤4 active Program Engagement)
3. Pricing meets floor?
4. Mission-aligned OR strategic bridge?
5. Can deliver excellence without depletion?

**5 yes → proceed. 3-4 → flag to COO. 0-2 → decline.**

**Polite decline:**
"Thank you for thinking of Rubinstein Productions. At the moment, this isn't quite the right fit — [capacity / scope / budget / alignment]. I'd love to stay in touch as the right opportunity emerges."
