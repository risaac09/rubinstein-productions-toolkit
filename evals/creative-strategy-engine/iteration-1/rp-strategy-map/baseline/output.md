# Creative Strategy Matrix — Rubinstein Productions
## Target: Executive Directors of Small Nonprofits | Offer: Mirror-Tier Single-Session ($900–$1,800)

---

## 1. Target Audience Definition

**Who:** Executive Directors of small nonprofits (budget $250K–$2M, staff 2–15, typically founder-led or second-ED)
**What they're considering:** A single Mirror-tier facilitated session — narrative clarification, board/staff alignment, or voice-liberation work — not a long engagement
**Price sensitivity:** $900–$1,800 is a real line item for them; must be justified against grant writing, retreats, or coaching alternatives
**Decision pattern:** ED decides solo or with a board chair; often uses discretionary/professional-development budget or a small restricted gift

---

## 2. Core Pains (ranked by acuteness)

| # | Pain | Surface language (how they say it) | Underlying condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | **Mission drift / story erosion** | "We can't articulate what we do anymore" / "Every funder hears a different version" | Founder story has fossilized; team can't inhabit it |
| P2 | **Board-staff misalignment** | "My board wants one thing, my team hears another" | No shared narrative spine; meetings are translation work |
| P3 | **Founder/ED burnout from carrying the story alone** | "I'm the only one who can tell it right" | ED is the single point of narrative failure |
| P4 | **Grant/pitch fatigue** | "I'm rewriting the same case for support for the fifth time this year" | Core story isn't portable across contexts |
| P5 | **Staff turnover erasing institutional voice** | "Every new hire re-invents how we talk about ourselves" | No embodied narrative onboarding |
| P6 | **Imposter/legitimacy fear at the funder table** | "We sound small" / "We sound like everyone else" | Voice is borrowed, not claimed |
| P7 | **Retreat/planning sessions that don't stick** | "We did a strategic plan and nothing changed" | Cognitive alignment without somatic/relational alignment |
| P8 | **Post-crisis narrative reset needed** | "After [scandal/loss/pivot] we don't know who we are now" | Old story broke; new one hasn't formed |

---

## 3. Personas (3 tiers of ED)

### Persona A — "The Story Carrier" (Founder-ED, 5–15 years in)
- **Profile:** Founded the org or inherited it early. Story lives in her body. Exhausted.
- **Dominant pains:** P1, P3, P6
- **Budget lens:** Will pay from discretionary funds if she believes the session will unburden her
- **Objection:** "I've done coaching. I've done retreats. Why is this different?"
- **What she needs to hear:** This is not a retreat. It's a mirror — you leave hearing your own voice, not mine.

### Persona B — "The Second Chapter ED" (2–4 years into a role she didn't found)
- **Profile:** Inherited a story that doesn't quite fit. Board still loyal to founder's language.
- **Dominant pains:** P2, P5, P8
- **Budget lens:** Needs board buy-in; must frame as alignment tool, not ED self-work
- **Objection:** "My board will ask what the deliverable is."
- **What she needs to hear:** You walk out with language the board can use. A single session that makes your next board meeting measurably different.

### Persona C — "The Pitch-Fatigued Operator" (ED running a lean, grant-dependent org)
- **Profile:** Spends 40%+ of her time writing the same case for support in slightly different words.
- **Dominant pains:** P4, P6, P7
- **Budget lens:** Sees this as capacity investment — one session saves 20 hours of future writing
- **Objection:** "$1,500 is a grant proposal's worth of my time."
- **What she needs to hear:** One session produces a story-spine you reuse in every proposal. Amortized over a year, it's $125/month.

| Angle | Claim | Best for | Evidence/proof it needs |

| **A8 — Not a Retreat** | Retreats give you alignment that evaporates. This gives you language that sticks in the body. | Persona A | Polyvagal/somatic differentiation |

| Stage | What they know | What they need | Angles to use | Channel/format |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | Don't know "voice liberation" exists; feel vague narrative fatigue | Name the pain | A3 (Say The Thing), A4 (Unburden) | Long-form essay, LinkedIn post, podcast guest |
| **Problem-aware** | Know they have a story problem; think it's a marketing/branding issue | Reframe: it's not branding, it's voice | A1 (Mirror), A8 (Not a Retreat) | Case study, workshop teaser, nonprofit-sector newsletter |

| **Product-aware** | Know RP exists; weighing Mirror-tier vs. other options | Remove friction; justify single-session price | A7 (Amortized), A5 (90 Min), testimonials | Direct CTA page, sample session outcome, booking link |
| **Most Aware** | Ready to book; need nudge on timing/format | Urgency without manipulation | A3, A7, capacity scarcity (real) | Email from Isaac, direct Calendly, referral intro |

## 7. Recommended First Creative Tests (Mirror-tier funnel)

1. **LinkedIn long-form (Unaware → Problem-aware):** Essay titled "The Thing You Keep Almost Saying in Board Meetings" — A3 angle, Persona A/B.
2. **Case study PDF (Solution-aware):** Single-session before/after with a named small nonprofit — A2 + A5.
3. **Discovery-call landing page (Product-aware):** A7 hour-math + testimonial carousel, single CTA to 20-min fit call.
4. **Sector-newsletter guest post (Problem-aware):** Pitch to a regional nonprofit association newsletter — A8 (Not a Retreat) as the differentiator.
5. **Warm referral script (Most Aware):** Short email template for past clients to forward to EDs in their network.

---

## 8. Guardrails & Notes

- **Constraint-as-product:** Mirror-tier is deliberately single-session. Don't let sales pressure expand it into a package — that collapses the offer's clarity.
- **Price anchor:** $900 is the floor (smaller orgs, first-time), $1,800 is the ceiling (larger boards, on-site). Stay in band.
- **Wabi-sabi voice:** Avoid nonprofit-consultant jargon ("alignment workshop," "strategic visioning"). Use Isaac's relational/somatic vocabulary without lapsing into woo.
- **Capacity:** Mirror-tier sessions should be capped per month (COO skill sets the number). Messaging scarcity must be real, not manufactured.
- **Next step after Mirror:** Natural upsell is a multi-session arc, but the Mirror must stand alone as a complete deliverable first.
