# Reach-outs for the week

## 1. Priya Rao — reach out Monday or Tuesday

**Why her, why now:** Three weeks is exactly the window where a warm coffee either becomes a collaboration or quietly fades. She named a specific problem (co-founder stuck on narrative) that sits directly in your wheelhouse. The follow-up is overdue but not yet awkward — waiting another week tips it toward awkward.

**Angle:** Lead with the specific thing she mentioned, not with generic "great to see you." Offer a concrete next step, not an open-ended "let me know if I can help."

**Draft opener:**
> Priya — been thinking about what you said about [co-founder's name] being stuck on the narrative piece. I ran into a similar knot with a client last month and have a framing that might unlock it. Want me to hop on a 30-min call with the two of you this week? No pitch, just curious whether the pattern matches. Thursday or Friday afternoon work?

**Why this works:** Specific reference proves you listened. Concrete offer (30 min, this week, two days named) makes it easy to say yes. "No pitch" removes the transactional undertone. This is exactly the kind of low-stakes generosity that turns collaborators into referrers.

## 2. Maya Okonkwo — reach out mid-week, no agenda

**Why her, why now:** A baby in January means she's ~3 months postpartum — deep in the fog, likely hearing from very few people who aren't asking her for something. A past client who had a great Mirror delivery is a warm relationship you want to keep warm, and this is a rare window where a no-ask check-in lands as genuine care rather than CRM hygiene.

**Angle:** Congratulations + zero ask. Do not mention work, referrals, or "when you're back." The whole point is that this one isn't about you.

**Draft opener:**
> Maya — I realized I never properly said congratulations on the baby. I hope you're getting stretches of sleep and that the people around you are showing up. No need to reply — just wanted you to know I'm thinking of you. The Mirror work we did together still comes up in my head a lot.

**Why this works:** The "no need to reply" is the whole gift — it removes the cognitive load of owing you a response. The closing line about Mirror is honest and specific without being a hook. Six months from now when she's thinking about her next project, this message will be why she texts you first.

## 3. David Lin — reach out this week, low-key

**Why him, why now:** Boston is April 20 this year, so he's in the final taper weeks — the point in a training cycle where runners get squirrelly, injury-paranoid, and isolated. Your worry is data. The fact that you haven't seen him + you're worried = exactly the moment a running buddy reaches out. Don't overthink this one.

**Angle:** Short, specific, no performance of concern. Offer a concrete low-cost invitation — a shakeout run, a coffee, walking him to the start line energetically.

**Draft opener (text him, don't email):**
> Hey man — Boston's what, two weeks out? Been thinking about you. Want to grab a shakeout run this weekend or next? I'll match your pace, no pushing. Or coffee if legs are off-limits.

**Why this works:** Texting (not emailing) matches the relationship. "I'll match your pace" removes the competitive edge taper-runners dread. Offering coffee as a backup removes pressure. You're not asking him to process anything — you're just showing up in his peripheral vision during the lonely part of the cycle.
