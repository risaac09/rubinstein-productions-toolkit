Sarah has spent about twelve years running programs for communities that most funders overlook. She started in direct service, moved into program design, and now leads a nonprofit where she builds the things she used to wish existed when she was doing the ground-level work.

She is good at the operational side, the budgets and the timelines. But colleagues tend to bring up something else first: she listens in a way that changes what gets built. Her equity work is specific. She redesigned her org's intake process after realizing the old one was filtering out the people it was supposed to serve.

She has been recognized in the nonprofit sector -- a couple of "40 under 40" lists, some speaking invitations. She seems more interested in whether the programs are actually reaching anyone than in the accolades.
