The tools keep changing but the problem stays the same: people need to actually talk to each other. What works isn't some silver-bullet method. It's showing up, listening harder than feels comfortable, and building trust slow enough that it holds weight. The best teams I've seen don't "leverage methodologies" — they argue well, repair fast, and stay honest when it costs them something. That's not simple. But it's the only version of progress that doesn't collapse the moment conditions shift.
