Technology changes how people work together. That part is obvious. The harder question is whether any particular method actually improves the working relationship or just adds process.

I think the useful stuff comes down to something boring: talk to people honestly, build trust over time, and pick tools that solve a real problem instead of sounding impressive in a slide deck. Most organizations already know this. They just keep buying software instead of doing it.

The work is slow and sometimes tedious. Worth doing, though, if you can resist the urge to call it transformative.
