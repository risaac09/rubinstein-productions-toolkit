## Questions / prompts to prep

Since you're giving her the 3 prompts on the day, the prep work is in the prompt design itself. For a Mirror session 2, the prompts should:

1. **Build on what surfaced in session 1.** Go back to session 1 footage/notes. What did she circle around? What did she almost say but not quite land? Session 2 prompts should escort her toward the thing she was near last time.

2. **Target different registers.** One prompt for conviction (the thing she knows cold), one for tenderness (the thing underneath the conviction), one for friction (the thing she's still working out). This gives you range in the edit.

3. **Be specific, not abstract.** "Tell me about the moment you decided to start Bright Current" beats "What is Bright Current about." Specificity unlocks presence.

**Things to confirm or decide before Thursday:**
- What are the 3 prompts? Draft them, sit with them overnight, revise Wednesday.
- What's the core question session 2 is answering? (The arc needs an answer, not just footage.)
- What do you want her wearing? (Continuity with session 1 or intentional contrast?)
- What's the deliverable from this session? (Stand-alone piece? Part of a composite?)

## Call-time logistics

### Arrival
- Target arrival: **60-75 min before first take.** Solo op needs buffer.
- Confirm parking / entry with Ama Wednesday.
- Ask about pets, housemates, neighbors, delivery schedule (audio killers).

### Setup sequence (solo workflow)
1. Walk the space first — find the frame before unpacking anything
2. Set camera position, lock tripod
3. Lights next (key, then fill, then negative fill)
4. Audio last — mic her once lights are set so her blocking is final
5. Test recording: 30 seconds of her talking normally, play back through headphones
6. Room tone: 60 seconds of silence, rolling

### Shoot flow
- Warm-up / check-in with Ama (15 min, off-camera) — she's in session 2, you have rapport, use it
- Prompt 1, 2-3 takes
- Reset / water / breathe
- Prompt 2, 2-3 takes
- Prompt 3, 2-3 takes
- Safety / b-roll of her workspace, hands, details

### Wrap
- Same-day backup: cards → SSD before you leave her house, or immediately on return home
- Quick debrief with Ama: how did that feel? (Informs session 3 design.)

## What to send Ama Thursday

A single email, warm and specific, covering:

**Subject:** Friday shoot — a few things to hold

**Body should include:**
1. **Confirmation:** arrival time, expected duration (total block she should hold)
2. **What to wear:** solid colors, avoid busy patterns, continuity with session 1 if relevant
3. **Space prep:** ask her to clear the frame area but keep the office feeling like hers (a few personal objects matter), turn off HVAC/fans if possible during takes, silence phones
4. **What to expect:** 3 prompts, she'll see them for the first time on the day (this is intentional — presence over preparation), multiple takes per prompt, ~90-120 min total
5. **What she doesn't need to do:** memorize, rehearse, or prepare answers. Come rested, come hydrated, come as herself.
6. **Posture frame:** this is session 2 — remind her of the arc, what session 1 opened, what this session is reaching toward
7. **Logistics:** your cell, what to do if anything comes up before Friday

Keep it under 250 words. She's a founder — respect her inbox.
