# 30-Minute Discovery Interview: Say Why Facilitation for Nonprofit EDs

## Purpose

Validate whether nonprofit Executive Directors have a real, urgent, funded problem that "Say Why facilitation" could plausibly solve. You are testing whether a **job** exists in their world — not selling the offering. Keep your idea out of their mouth until the final minutes (if at all).

## Ground rules for you (the interviewer)

1. **Talk about their life, not your idea.** Don't describe Say Why until the very end, and only if they ask.
2. **Ask about the past and the specific, not the future and the hypothetical.** "Tell me about the last time…" beats "Would you ever…".
3. **Talk less, listen more.** Target 20% you, 80% them.
4. **Dig for currency:** time spent, money spent, workarounds built, people pulled in, consequences suffered. Compliments and "that sounds interesting" are worthless signals.
5. **Watch for anti-signals:** vague enthusiasm, "we should really do that someday," no budget owner, no prior attempts.

## Pre-interview prep (5 min before the call)

- Look up the org's 990, recent annual report, last board meeting minutes if public.
- Note: budget size, staff count, mission area, recent transitions (new ED, layoffs, merger, capital campaign).
- Prepare one custom question per ED based on something specific to their org.

## The 30-minute structure

### 0:00–2:00 — Frame & consent (2 min)

> "Thanks for the time. I'm doing research on what it's actually like to lead a nonprofit right now — specifically around communication, voice, and the hard conversations EDs have to navigate. I'm not selling anything today. I'd love to just hear your story. Is it okay if I take notes / record for my own reference?"

- Confirm role, tenure, org size, staff count.
- Confirm: "What does a typical week look like for you right now?"

### 2:00–8:00 — Current state & pain landscape (6 min)

Open wide. Find where the heat is.

1. "What's the hardest part of your job in the last 3 months?"
2. "When you think about your own leadership, where do you feel most stuck or most watched?"
3. "Walk me through the last time you had to say something hard — to a funder, board, staff, or the public. What happened?"
4. "Where do you feel you can't fully say what you actually think? Who are you performing for?"

*(Listen for: silencing, self-censorship, board friction, funder power dynamics, founder voice vs. org voice, fear of media/public, DEI conversations, succession, burnout.)*

### 8:00–16:00 — Specific past incident deep-dive (8 min)

Pick the juiciest thread from the previous section. Go narrow and concrete.

5. "Tell me about a specific moment in the last year when you felt you couldn't speak freely — or spoke and regretted it. What was at stake?"
6. "What did you do? Who did you talk to? What did it cost you — time, sleep, political capital?"
7. "Did you bring anyone in to help you think it through? A coach, a peer ED, a consultant, a therapist, your board chair?"
8. "What did that help cost? Was it worth it? What was missing?"
9. "How did it resolve — or is it still unresolved?"

*(This is your Mom Test gold. You're looking for: active pain, money already spent, workarounds, unmet needs.)*

### 16:00–22:00 — Workarounds, spend, and decision-making (6 min)

Quantify the problem.

10. "What are you doing right now to handle this kind of thing? Coaching? Peer groups? Drafting and re-drafting? Avoiding it?"
11. "In the last 12 months, how much have you spent — money or staff time — on executive coaching, comms consultants, facilitators, board dev, DEI consultants, or similar?"
12. "Who decides that spend? You? Board? A specific budget line?"
13. "When you hired [that coach / consultant / facilitator], what made you pull the trigger? What were you hoping would change?"
14. "What did you stop using, and why?"

*(Listen for: active budget, named vendors, recent dissatisfaction, unmet need, internal champion.)*

### 22:00–27:00 — Ideal world & stakes (5 min)

15. "If this whole area got solved, what would be different for you, your staff, your org?"
16. "What happens if nothing changes in the next year?"
17. "Who else in your world is wrestling with this same thing? Other EDs you'd point me to?"
    *(Referral ask — captures network even if they're not the buyer.)*

### 27:00–30:00 — Reveal & react (3 min, optional)

Only now, if the conversation warrants it:

> "I'll share briefly what I'm exploring, and I'd love your honest reaction — especially if it sounds off. I work with leaders on what I call *Say Why* — helping them find and speak their actual truth in high-stakes settings where they normally self-edit. [One sentence on modality — facilitation, somatic, narrative — whatever is true.] Does any of that land, or miss, given what you just told me?"

18. "What would you need to see to believe it works?"
19. "What would make you not hire someone like me?"

Close:
> "This was incredibly helpful. Can I come back to you in a few weeks with what I'm learning? And — would you be open to introducing me to one or two other EDs you mentioned?"

## Signals to weigh after all 3 interviews

**Strong validation:**
- 2+ EDs describe a specific, recent, painful incident unprompted.
- They've already spent money on adjacent services (coaching, comms, facilitation) in the last 12 months.
- They have a named budget line or decision authority.
- They offer referrals without prompting.
- They ask *you* questions at the end about working together.

**Weak / no validation:**
- "That sounds interesting" with no specific incident.
- No prior spend, no workaround, no named vendor.
- Pain lives in staff or board, not the ED themselves.
- They reframe your topic into something else they actually care about (listen to that — it may be the real job).

## After the 3 interviews

Write a one-page synthesis answering:
1. What job, in their words, are they trying to get done?
2. What are they currently hiring to do that job, and where is that failing?
3. Is there active budget, an active buyer, and active pain?
4. Does "Say Why facilitation" map onto that job — or do you need to rename, reframe, or reshape the offering?
