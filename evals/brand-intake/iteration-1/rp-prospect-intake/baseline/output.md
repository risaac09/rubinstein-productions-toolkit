# Brand Intake — Harbor Light Coalition

Welcome. Before we talk scope, deliverables, or budget, I want to understand who you are, who you serve, and what you're actually trying to move. This intake is designed to surface the raw material we'll need to do real creative work together — not a questionnaire to fill out and forget.

We'll work through this in five sections. Take your time. The more specific you can be, the better the work gets downstream.

---

## Section 1 — Organization & Mission

**1.1** In one or two sentences, what does Harbor Light Coalition do? (Not the formal mission statement — how you'd describe it to a friend at a dinner party.)

**1.2** When and why was Harbor Light founded? What problem was someone looking at in Providence that made this organization necessary?

**1.3** Who are the people behind Harbor Light — executive director, board chair, clinical lead, founding voices? Whose vision is this organization carrying?

**1.4** What's your 501(c)(3) status, budget range, and staff size? (Rough numbers are fine — I want to calibrate scale, not audit you.)

**1.5** What are you NOT? What do people sometimes assume Harbor Light does that you have to correct?

---

## Section 2 — Who You Serve

**2.1** Describe the person Harbor Light most serves. Age, lived experience, what they're navigating, where in Providence/RI they're located. A real composite, not a demographic slice.

**2.2** What does someone feel in the moment they decide to reach out to Harbor Light? What pushed them to the edge where they picked up the phone or walked in?

**2.3** What are the top 2–3 barriers your community faces in accessing mental health care? (Cost, stigma, language, cultural fit, wait times, trauma with prior providers — be honest.)

**2.4** Who else do you serve beyond direct clients? Families, first responders, schools, referring clinicians, funders, policy makers?

**2.5** Where does your community currently gather — physically and online? What do they trust?

---

## Section 3 — Programs, Services & Proof

**3.1** Walk me through your core programs or services. What does someone actually experience when they engage with Harbor Light?

**3.2** What's working? Give me one specific story (anonymized is fine) of a person or family whose trajectory changed because of Harbor Light.

**3.3** What outcomes do you track? What do you wish you could track but can't yet?

**3.4** What's a myth about mental health care — or about your specific community — that you're constantly having to counter?

**3.5** Who are the other organizations in the RI mental health ecosystem doing adjacent work? Who do you collaborate with, refer to, learn from?

---

## Section 4 — Voice, Values & Non-Negotiables

**4.1** If Harbor Light were a person, how would they speak? Pick three adjectives and tell me what each one rules out.

**4.2** What words or framings do you refuse to use? (e.g. "consumers," "mental illness," "at-risk," "saved" — whatever makes you wince.)

**4.3** What words or framings are essential to how you describe the work? (e.g. "coalition," "lived experience," "co-regulation," "accessible.")

**4.4** Show me one piece of communication — from anyone, anywhere — that made you think "yes, that's the tone we want." And one that made you think "never that."

**4.5** What are your non-negotiables around representing clients? Consent, anonymity, compensation for sharing stories, showing faces, showing struggle?

---

## Section 5 — The Ask (What Brought You Here)

**5.1** What triggered you reaching out to Rubinstein Productions right now? A grant deadline, a capital campaign, a rebrand, a specific video need, a board mandate?

**5.2** What does success look like 90 days from now? 12 months from now?

**5.3** What have you tried before — internally or with other partners — and what did or didn't work?

**5.4** What's the budget range you're working within for this engagement? (A range is fine. I'd rather know than guess.)

**5.5** Who are the decision-makers on your side, and what's their appetite for iteration vs. a single polished deliverable?

---

## Next Steps

Once you send these back — written, voice memo, Loom, whatever's easiest — I'll synthesize a brand brief and we'll schedule a 45-minute working call to pressure-test it together. From there we move into creative strategy and scope.

No rush. Respond in the form that serves you.
