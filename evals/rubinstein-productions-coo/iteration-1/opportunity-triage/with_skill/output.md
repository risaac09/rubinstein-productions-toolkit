# Opportunity Triage: Boston Environmental Nonprofit Brand Video

## Classification: B — Adjacent

This is not an A. It is not a C either. Here is the breakdown.

---

## What is right about this opportunity

- **Segment match:** Nonprofit ED is a documented audience segment. Environmental nonprofit in Boston is squarely in the mission-driven organization lane.
- **ED engagement:** The ED is personally engaged, not delegating to a comms coordinator. That matters — Say Why work requires principal-level participation.
- **Sector fit:** Environmental nonprofits often sit at exactly the gap RP addresses: they know what is true about their mission but their public expression sounds like every other org in the space.

## What is wrong about this opportunity

- **Framing mismatch:** The ED keeps calling it "a marketing video." This is the central flag. RP is not a video production company. The camera is a witness, not a polishing tool. If the client wants a deliverable-first engagement — a 3-minute product they can post — without the excavation work underneath it, you will end up in a production vendor role. That role pays worse, drains more energy, and reinforces the wrong positioning for RP.
- **Budget is ambiguous at best:** $2,500 lands in the Mirror tier range ($500-$1,500 per session, or $2,500-$4,000 for a 3-session arc). But a "3-minute brand video" with filming, editing, and delivery is not a Mirror session — it is a Map-level engagement with production labor attached. A Map half-day starts at $3,000-$5,000; a full sprint at $6,000-$12,000. At $2,500 for what they are describing, you would be working below floor rate once you account for filming (24-hr prep + 5-day post-latency minimum), editing, and revision cycles.
- **Scope creep risk is high:** "Brand video" without a narrative excavation process underneath it will generate revision loops. The client will keep asking for changes because neither of you will have done the work to know what the video should actually say. This is the classic pattern: production without diagnosis leads to infinite tinkering.

## The math

A 3-minute video engagement realistically requires:
- Discovery/excavation session: 2-3 hours
- Pre-production/prep: half day minimum
- Filming session: half to full day + 24-hr advance prep
- Post-production: 2-3 days minimum
- 5-day latency before delivery (Generator energy principle)
- At least one revision pass

Conservative estimate: 25-35 hours of work across 2-3 weeks. At $2,500 that is $70-$100/hour. Your floor is $500/hour. This is not close.

## Recommendation: Counter-propose

Do not take this as scoped. Do not pass on it entirely. The segment fit and ED engagement are real signals. Here is what to do:

**1. Reframe the engagement.** The conversation to have: "I do not make marketing videos. What I do is help organizations like yours recover the voice underneath the institutional script — and one output of that process can be a video artifact. But the video is the last step, not the first. Let me share what that process looks like."

**2. Propose a Map-level engagement.** A half-day Say The Thing package at $5,000-$6,000 that includes:
- A Voice Excavation session with the ED (and key team if appropriate)
- Narrative strategy output (what should the video actually say, from the inside out)
- One filmed session using the excavated material
- A 3-minute edited artifact as a deliverable

This respects the pricing floor, positions RP correctly as the narrative practice rather than the production vendor, and gives the client something better than what they asked for.

**3. If budget is hard-capped at $2,500**, offer a Mirror-tier engagement only — a single Voice Excavation session or a 2-session arc. No video production included. The session itself may produce footage that could become something, but you are not contracting for a finished video at that price. Be direct about why: "At that budget I can offer the excavation work, which is the most valuable part. The production side requires a different scope and investment."

**4. Test the Sacral response.** Before sending any proposal — does this feel like a "hell yes" or are you thinking yourself into it because the sector is interesting? If it is a "probably," trust that. The right environmental nonprofit client will come at the right price.

## One more thing

The fact that the ED "keeps calling it a marketing video" is actually diagnostic information. It tells you the organization is experiencing exactly the problem RP solves — they are defaulting to the institutional register ("marketing video") because they do not yet have language for what they actually need (narrative recovery, voice excavation). That is not a reason to decline. It is a reason to lead the conversation rather than follow their framing.
