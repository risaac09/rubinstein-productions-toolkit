# Voice Liberation — Sticky Tagline

## Recommended Tagline

**"Stop sounding like your industry. Start sounding like you."**

---

## Runner-Up Options (ranked)

1. **"Stop sounding like your industry. Start sounding like you."** — the lead recommendation.
2. **"Your sector has a voice. It isn't yours."**
3. **"Leaders don't need a better script. They need their own."**
4. **"The day you stop quoting your industry is the day people start quoting you."**
5. **"Say the thing only you can say."**

---

## SUCCESs Scorecard — Why #1 Wins

| Principle | Score | Why |
|-----------|-------|-----|
| **Simple** | 9/10 | Two verbs, one pivot. Commander's Intent: *stop the mimicry, start the self.* A smart 12-year-old gets it. |
| **Unexpected** | 9/10 | Violates the default leadership-coaching promise ("we'll polish your message"). The surprise: the problem isn't skill — it's camouflage. |
| **Concrete** | 8/10 | "Your industry" is a specific, picturable thing (the LinkedIn-speak, the panel-jargon, the same five words every CEO in the sector uses). "You" is the most concrete noun a person owns. |
| **Credible** | 8/10 | Self-evident on contact. Everyone has sat in a meeting and thought *this person is reading from the sector's script.* The tagline passes the "I've felt this" test. |
| **Emotional** | 10/10 | Hits identity directly — the core Maslow layer (esteem, self-actualization). Names a quiet shame leaders carry: *I don't sound like me anymore.* |
| **Stories** | 8/10 | Implies a before/after narrative: the leader who finally dropped the sector costume and was heard for the first time. Ready-made story frame. |

**Total: 52/60** — extremely sticky range.

---

## Why This Works (Curse-of-Knowledge check)

- **No jargon.** No "authentic," no "executive presence," no "narrative alignment." Those are sector words — the exact thing the tagline is against.
- **Parallel structure** ("Stop X. Start Y.") creates rhythm and memorability. The brain files it as one chunk.
- **Identity framing, not feature framing.** It's not about what you'll learn — it's about who you stop being and who you become.
- **The pivot is the product.** The tagline IS the methodology in miniature: naming the borrowed voice, then returning to the original one.

---

## Usage Notes

- **Website hero:** Works as-is above the fold.
- **Email signature / bio line:** Use #5 ("Say the thing only you can say.") — shorter, more intriguing in isolation.
- **Pitch deck cover:** Use #2 ("Your sector has a voice. It isn't yours.") — creates a curiosity gap that demands page 2.
- **Case-study opener:** Use #4 — sets up the transformation arc.
