# Rubinstein Productions One-Liner

## Primary One-Liner

**We help nonprofit leaders and mission-driven founders who sound like everyone else in their sector find their real voice through facilitated conversations and brand video, so their message finally lands with the people they're meant to reach.**

## Formula Breakdown

- **Character:** nonprofit leaders and mission-driven founders
- **Problem (external + internal):** sounding like everyone else in their sector (external) / the frustration of not being heard and watching your message get lost in the noise (internal)
- **Solution:** find their real voice through facilitated conversations and brand video
- **Result:** their message finally lands with the people they're meant to reach

## Variations (for different contexts)

**Shorter, conversational (networking / elevator):**
> We help nonprofit EDs and mission-driven founders stop sounding like everyone else in their sector — so their real voice comes through on camera and in the room.

**Homepage hero version:**
> You're doing work that matters. But your message sounds like every other org in your space. We help mission-driven leaders find their real voice — through facilitated conversation, narrative work, and brand video — so the right people can actually hear you.

**Problem-first version (for cold outreach):**
> Most nonprofit and mission-driven messaging sounds interchangeable. We help leaders surface the voice underneath the sector jargon, then carry it into brand video that actually sounds like them.

## Score: 9/10

**Strengths:**
- Customer is clearly the hero ("we help [you]")
- Names the specific external problem ("sounding like everyone else")
- Implies the internal problem (the frustration of being unheard / invisible)
- Specific character (not "everyone") — nonprofit EDs and mission-driven founders
- Solution is concrete (facilitated conversations, brand video, narrative work)
- Result closes the story gap (being heard by the right people)
- Uses the customer's likely language, not jargon

**To reach 10/10:**
- Field-test with 3-5 actual nonprofit EDs: can they repeat it after hearing it once? If "real voice" feels abstract to them, swap in their words (e.g., "sound like yourself" or "stop sounding like a grant application").
- Consider A/B testing "find their real voice" vs. "stop sounding like a grant application" — the latter is more concrete and pattern-interrupting, which scores higher on clarity.
