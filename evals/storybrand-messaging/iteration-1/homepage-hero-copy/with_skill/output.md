## SB7 Element Map (for the recommended hero)

| SB7 Element | Where it lives |
|---|---|
| **1. Character + Desire** | "Your mission deserves to be heard in your own voice." — names the ED as hero; desire = being heard authentically. |
| **2. Problem** | External: sector-generic messaging. Internal: you sound like everyone else / you've lost your own voice. Philosophical: mission-driven leaders shouldn't have to perform the sector to be taken seriously. Villain: sector-speak / jargon machine. |
| **3. Guide** | "We help executive directors reclaim…" — empathy (we see the grinding-down) + implicit authority (clear POV: Say Why, named methodology). Authority proof bar lives directly under the hero (client logos, film stills, outcomes). |
| **4. Plan** | Appears in the section below the hero as a 3-step process: 1) Discovery conversation 2) Say Why engagement (facilitation / narrative / film, scoped to your org) 3) Assets + skills you keep. |
| **5. CTA** | Direct: "Book a Discovery Call." Transitional: "See the Say Why approach." |
| **6. Failure stakes** | Implied in the subhead ("ground down into sector-speak… sounds like everyone else's") and stated explicitly in the stakes section below: another year of comms that don't sound like you, donors who can't remember what makes you different. |
| **7. Success** | Reclaimed language, on-camera presence, a narrative that moves funders and staff — expanded in the success section below the fold. |

## Self-score: 9/10

**What's working:**
- ED is clearly the hero; RP is the guide.
- Opens a real story gap (performing the sector → speaking authentically).
- Names internal problem (sounding like everyone else, losing your own voice), not just external.
- Villain is specific and relatable (sector-speak, jargon machine) rather than abstract.
- One clear desire per headline; no list of services in the H1.
- Direct + transitional CTA both present.
- Say Why is positioned as a named methodology, which signals authority without an origin-story detour.

**To reach 10/10 (needs real assets, not copy changes):**
- Add an authority proof strip directly under the hero: 2–3 client logos or names, one outcome stat, and a short testimonial from a nonprofit ED in the hero's own language. Without visible authority, empathy alone reads as weak.
- Confirm "Say Why" is defined in one short sentence visible on the page within the first scroll — otherwise it's a term the visitor has to decode.
