# Topic Recommendation: What RP Should Write About Next

## The signal, read plainly

Two adjacent conversations are converging in RP's field:

- **Voice authenticity under AI pressure** (4 accounts, 10 days) — a concern, not a celebration. People are watching distinctiveness flatten.
- **The cost of safe messaging** (3 accounts) — nonprofits sanding themselves into irrelevance to avoid risk.
- **2 pipeline prospects amplified the first thread** — this isn't just ambient noise; it's resonating with people who are already considering hiring RP.

These aren't two topics. They're one topic seen from two angles: **what gets lost when organizations optimize for safety — whether the safety is algorithmic (AI smoothing) or reputational (board-approved blandness).**

## What RP should write about next

**Working title: "The Thing You Won't Say Is The Thing That Would Work"**

(Alt: "Safe Messaging Is The Most Expensive Thing You're Buying")

### Why this angle, specifically

RP's whole methodology — "Say The Thing," polyvagal co-regulation, relational ontology — is a direct answer to this exact anxiety. The field is asking a question RP already has a practiced response to. That's a rare alignment and worth moving on quickly while the conversation is hot.

But don't write the generic "AI can't replace human voice" take. That's what the 4 accounts already wrote, and it's becoming its own flavor of safe messaging. Instead, write the version only RP can write:

**The two safety-seeking behaviors have the same root cause and the same cost.** Nonprofits self-censoring and brands AI-smoothing are both doing the same thing: outsourcing the nervous-system work of being distinct. Both produce content that can't co-regulate with an audience because there's no regulated nervous system behind it. Both are symptoms of organizations that haven't done the internal work to know what they actually believe.

The AI-voice conversation and the safe-messaging conversation are the same conversation. Name that.

### Concrete hooks to try

1. **"Your voice isn't being stolen by AI. It's being abandoned by your approval process."** — reframes the AI-panic as a symptom of a pre-existing condition.
2. **"Safe messaging and AI-generated content are the same product."** — both are risk-averse outputs from systems that won't metabolize conflict.
3. **"The nonprofits worried about AI flattening their voice already flattened it in the last board meeting."** — sharp, names the hypocrisy without being cruel.
4. **"There's no prompt that makes a regulated nervous system. That's the moat."** — positive framing; positions RP's methodology as the actual unfakeable thing.

### Format recommendation

- **Primary piece:** One longer-form post (LinkedIn article or newsletter, 600-900 words) that does the both-are-one-thing synthesis. This is the piece the 2 prospects can forward to their boards.
- **Social atomization:** 3-4 shorter IG/LinkedIn posts pulling individual hooks. Prioritize the "same product" reframe — it's the one the 4 watched accounts haven't said.
- **Optional follow-up:** A practical piece on "what it actually takes to say the thing" — walks through RP's methodology as the answer. Don't lead with this; earn it with the diagnostic piece first.

### Why now, not later

- The topic is peaking — 7 posts in 10 days across watched accounts is a live wave.
- 2 pipeline prospects are already emotionally engaged with the thread. A well-positioned piece from RP gives them language to explain to their own stakeholders why they're considering RP. That's pipeline acceleration, not just content.
- Waiting even a week risks the topic going stale or getting saturated with reactive, undifferentiated takes.

### What to avoid

- Don't bash AI. The sophisticated version of this conversation isn't anti-AI; it's about what AI reveals about organizations that were already hollow.
- Don't be defensive about RP's methodology. Lead with the diagnosis; let readers arrive at RP as the answer.
- Don't make it a long manifesto. The thread is moving. Ship the sharp version this week.

## One-line answer

**Write the piece that merges both threads: "Self-censorship and AI-generated content are the same safety behavior, and they have the same cost — a voice no one can co-regulate with."** Ship within 5 days while the conversation is live and the pipeline prospects are warm.
