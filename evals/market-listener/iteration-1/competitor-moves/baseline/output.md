# Competitor Moves: Synthesis & RP Response

## What's happening

Three signals, one pattern: **the voice/narrative consultancy space is consolidating around higher-ticket intensives and content-as-credentialing, and RP's pipeline is being actively courted by a direct competitor.**

### Signal 1: @namebearer_studio — the conceptual land-grab
A 5x-engagement video essay distinguishing "brand voice" vs. "institutional voice" is doing category-definition work. The 890 engagements matter less than *who* engaged: **3 prospects in RP's pipeline.** That's not ambient discovery — that's a competitor planting a flag in conceptual territory RP has lived in but hasn't publicly claimed. Namebearer is teaching RP's prospects a vocabulary before RP does. When those prospects get on a call with RP, they'll now be using Namebearer's frame.

**Threat level: high, but latent.** No direct poaching yet — this is upstream influence on how prospects think.

### Signal 2: @council_voice — direct pricing + positioning collision
"The Clearing" at $6k is a 4x markup on RP's Mirror tier ($1.5k) for what sounds like adjacent deliverables. Two reads:

- **Read A (threatening):** Council Voice has found price elasticity RP hasn't tested. They're anchoring the market higher, which could make Mirror look underpriced-but-also-under-credentialed ("why is theirs 4x? must be better").
- **Read B (opportunity):** $6k intensives create a tier gap RP can occupy. Council Voice has now made $1.5k look accessible rather than cheap. RP can either (a) hold Mirror at $1.5k as the accessible entry and add a premium tier above, or (b) raise Mirror toward $2.5-3k and let $1.5k become a lighter offering.

**Threat level: medium. Pricing signal is useful regardless of how RP responds.**

### Signal 3: @narrative_lab — content territory overlap + list-building
340 email signups in 48h on "writing from the work" is a strong funnel event. 60% territory overlap means: **every person on that list is a qualified lead for RP who is now being nurtured by someone else's sequence.** Narrative Lab is building a list RP could have built.

**Threat level: medium-high. This is ongoing — the list compounds.**

---

## The meta-pattern

All three competitors are doing **public pedagogy as positioning**. Namebearer teaches distinctions. Council Voice teaches via a productized intensive. Narrative Lab teaches via email course. RP's methodology ("Say The Thing," polyvagal co-regulation, relational ontology) is more differentiated than any of theirs — but it's largely *private*. The asymmetry: **RP has deeper IP and less public footprint.**

## Proposed RP response (prioritized)

### Priority 1 — Recapture the pipeline prospects (this week)
Those 3 prospects who engaged with Namebearer's video essay are now half-educated in a competitor's frame. Don't let the next touch be cold.

- Send a personal note to each referencing the video *obliquely* ("saw you thinking about institutional voice lately — here's how I'd complicate that distinction...")
- Offer a 20-min "voice audit" conversation, free, no pitch. This is relational recovery, not sales.
- **Owner: Isaac. Timeline: 48h.**

### Priority 2 — Publish RP's own category-defining piece (2 weeks)
Namebearer got 890 engagements on a distinction. RP has stronger distinctions sitting in methodology docs. Pick one and ship it.

Candidates:
- "Brand voice is a performance. Institutional voice is a nervous system." (Polyvagal reframe of Namebearer's exact topic — positions RP upstream of their frame.)
- "Why most voice work fails: it treats voice as output, not regulation."
- "Say The Thing: the difference between authentic and accurate."

Format: 4-6 min video essay OR long-form written piece. Published to IG + cross-posted to RP site. **Owner: Isaac drafts, content-publishing-agent ships.**

### Priority 3 — Pricing + tier response to Council Voice (next 30 days)
Don't reactively match $6k. Instead:
- Add a premium tier **above** Mirror — something like "Deep Mirror" or a longitudinal engagement at $4-5k that's structurally different from a single-session intensive (RP's edge is *relational continuity*, not single-session catharsis).
- Hold Mirror at $1.5k but reframe it in copy as "the entry point to relational voice work" — turning the price gap into a positioning feature.
- **Owner: Isaac + proposal-scope-builder skill.**

### Priority 4 — Build RP's own list-building funnel (60 days)
Narrative Lab's 340 signups is a wake-up call. RP has no comparable top-of-funnel. Ship a free asset that is *distinctly RP*:
- A 5-day email sequence on "Voice as Co-Regulation" — pulls from polyvagal methodology, can't be replicated by Narrative Lab.
- Or: a downloadable "Say The Thing" worksheet/self-audit tool.
- Gate it behind email signup. Nurture sequence points toward Mirror.
- **Owner: Isaac + outreach-email-manager skill for sequence, content-publishing-agent for distribution.**

### Priority 5 — Ongoing competitive listening (systemic)
Set up weekly 15-min review of these three accounts + 2-3 others. Track: new offerings, pricing changes, content that overperforms, RP-pipeline engagement. This should become a standing input to monthly planning.

## What NOT to do

- **Don't match Council Voice's $6k tier reactively.** Pricing should reflect RP's actual differentiation, not competitor anchoring.
- **Don't produce content that responds to Namebearer by name or references their framing.** Go upstream of them, don't react to them.
- **Don't rush a 7-email course to match Narrative Lab.** Their course works because it matches their voice. RP's funnel needs to feel like RP — slower, more somatic, more relational.

---

## Bottom line

RP is being out-marketed by competitors with weaker methodology. The response isn't to marketize harder — it's to **make RP's existing depth publicly legible.** One strong piece of pedagogy, one pricing/tier adjustment, and one owned funnel asset would close most of the gap these three signals reveal.

**First move this week: the 3 pipeline prospects.** Everything else can wait a beat.
