Filming a founder for her speaker reel last week. Between takes she dropped her shoulders and said, "I hate this. I hate hearing my own voice. I sound performative."

I said, "Yeah — because you're performing. Who are you talking TO in your head right now?"

She said, "The person hiring me."

I said, "Talk to your sister."

The next take was the one.

Here's what I keep noticing in this work: the camera doesn't make people performative. The imagined audience does. The minute you're pitching to the gatekeeper in your head — the hiring committee, the investor, the skeptic — your voice leaves your body. It goes up into your throat and gets thin. You start selecting words instead of meaning them.

The fix isn't "be more confident" or "relax." It's changing who you're actually talking to. Picture one real person who already loves you. Talk to them. The content doesn't change. The frequency does.

Most people don't need better messaging. They need to stop auditioning for ghosts.

That's the work.
