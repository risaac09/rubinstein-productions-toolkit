# Case Study Outline: Climate-Adaptation Nonprofit — Map-tier Engagement (4 weeks)

**Pillar:** Facilitation and Film / Narrative Systems Thinking
**Level:** Meal (long-form case study)
**Platform:** Portfolio / Website

---

## 1. Opening Hook
A climate-adaptation nonprofit came to us sounding like three different organizations in one trench coat — their website, their grant narratives, and their tenant-facing materials each spoke a different dialect. The funders noticed before they did.

## 2. Problem Framing
The condition wasn't a branding problem; it was structural narrative fragmentation — what happens when a mission-driven org scales faster than its voice can integrate. Different teams were each telling a true story, but the organization had no shared language for what it actually did.

## 3. Process
Over four weeks we ran a Map-tier engagement: stakeholder listening sessions across program, development, and tenant-services teams; narrative audit of existing materials; and facilitated synthesis toward a Bilingual Dashboard — the inner (diagnostic) language they used with themselves and the outer (methodological) language they used with funders and tenants.

## 4. Turning Point
The shift came mid-week-three, when the executive director read the draft voice guide aloud and said, "This is what I've been trying to say for two years." The org didn't need new language — they needed permission to stop code-switching between audiences and trust that one voice could hold the whole.

## 5. Outcome
We delivered a Bilingual Dashboard and a unified voice guide calibrated to their three audiences without collapsing them. In the 60 days following delivery, they landed $180K in new funding — not because the writing was flashier, but because funders could finally hear a coherent organization.

## 6. Reflection
Voice alignment isn't a comms deliverable; it's an integrity audit that happens to produce written artifacts. When an organization stops performing different versions of itself to different rooms, resources tend to arrive — because trust is the scarce thing, and coherence is how trust travels.
