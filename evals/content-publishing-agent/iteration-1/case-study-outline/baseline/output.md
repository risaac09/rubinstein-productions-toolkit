# Case Study: Climate-Adaptation Nonprofit — Bilingual Dashboard + Voice Guide

## 1. Opening Hook
A climate-adaptation nonprofit was doing urgent work on a warming coast, but their website, grant narratives, and tenant materials each sounded like a different organization — three voices competing for the same mission.

## 2. Problem Framing
Funders, tenants, and community partners were meeting three versions of the org depending on which door they walked through, and that fragmentation was quietly bleeding credibility at exactly the moment the work needed to scale.

## 3. Process
Over a four-week Map-tier engagement, we ran a voice audit across every artifact, held listening sessions with staff who actually wrote the tenant-facing materials, and surfaced the patterns where the real org was already speaking clearly.

## 4. Turning Point
The shift came when we stopped trying to invent a new voice and instead named the one already alive in their tenant-organizing work — that language became the anchor for both the English and Spanish sides of the dashboard.

## 5. Outcome
They left with a Bilingual Dashboard and a unified voice guide their whole team could use, and within 60 days of delivery they landed $180K in new funding that their previous materials hadn't been able to unlock.

## 6. Reflection
The lesson wasn't that they needed better writing — it was that their clearest voice was already inside the organization, buried under the assumption that each audience required a different performance.
