TO: jen@thresholdrs.com
SUBJECT: The translation problem

Jen,

I've been sitting with what you said Tuesday — that it isn't a message problem, it's a translation problem between what clinicians say in groups and what the website can hold under the weight of stigma and liability. That framing has stayed with me all week, because I think you named the actual thing, and I think most orgs in your position never get that far.

The board is asking for a refreshed message because the outside version feels thin compared to what they sense is happening inside. But refreshing the language won't close that gap — it'll just produce a more polished version of the same translation loss. The real work is building a bridge that can carry the clinical truth across the liability line without breaking it. That's a different project than a rebrand, and it's the one worth doing.

A few things I'm curious about: Where does the translation break down first — is it at intake, in the way outcomes get described, in how you talk about what actually happens in groups? And who on staff currently holds the "inside" version most clearly? Because that person is probably the source material, whether or not they know it yet.

If it'd be useful, I'd like to come back for a second conversation focused specifically on mapping where the translation fractures — not to pitch anything, just to think with you about whether there's a shape of work here that would actually help. Let me know if that feels like a yes.

— Isaac
