## Understanding Your Situation

What I'm hearing is a classic systemic alienation from honest expression pattern in mission-driven nonprofits carrying multiple audiences at once:

- **Grant voice** performs legibility for funders — outcomes-forward, data-anchored, often emotionally flattened to meet expectations of "seriousness."
- **Website voice** tries to be everything to everyone — board, press, donors, tenants, partners — and becomes no one.
- **Tenant-facing voice** is probably the truest, because it's closest to the actual work and the actual people, but it's rarely treated as the source.

When three voices compete for the same organizational body, audiences feel the dissonance even if they can't name it. Board members sense something is "off-brand." Funders sense generality. Tenants sense that the website isn't speaking to them. Staff second-guess what to say and how to say it, and communications becomes a bottleneck that runs through you.

The work isn't to pick one of the three voices and force the others into alignment. The work is to excavate the voice that already lives *underneath* all three — the one your staff uses in the hallway, the one a tenant organizer uses when the camera is off, the one Reina uses when she's telling a friend what Delta Housing actually does. That's the voice the other three are approximations of. Once it's named and held, the three channels stop competing. They become translations of a single source.

This is Say Why work, not brand strategy as usually defined. The deliverable is a narrative architecture your team can run without me.

## Proposed Engagement: Map Tier — Narrative Strategy Sprint

**Duration:** 4-day sprint + 2-week integration window
**Timeline:** Designed to complete 6 weeks before your fiscal year end, leaving buffer for board review and channel rollout

### Phase 1 — Excavation (Days 1–2, on-site or hybrid)

- **Voice-source interviews** with 5–7 people across Delta Housing: you, Executive Director, 1–2 program leads, 1–2 frontline staff, 1–2 tenant leaders or board members with lived experience. These are non-extractive sessions — I'm listening for the organization's actual voice, not harvesting soundbites.
- **Cross-channel audit:** I review your current website, last 2 grant narratives, 6–8 tenant-facing materials, and any recent board communications. I'm mapping the dysregulation, not scoring it.
- **Narrative dissonance map** — a visual artifact showing where the three voices diverge, what each is performing for, and where the true source voice leaks through.

### Phase 2 — Excavation Sessions (Day 3, group work)

- **Half-day facilitated session** with you plus 3–5 core staff/leadership. This is where the source voice gets named out loud, collectively, and held by the people who have to use it.
- Working with the camera as co-regulatory witness (optional — filmed segments can become internal rollout assets, no obligation). This is a Zero-Measurement Zone: no one is being performed at or evaluated. The output is clarity, not content.

### Phase 3 — Mapping (Day 4, synthesis)

- **Narrative Architecture document** (your Bilingual Dashboard deliverable):
  - Core voice principles — 4–6 statements naming how Delta Housing actually sounds
  - Tonal translation guide: how the source voice adapts (not fragments) into grant, website, and tenant-facing channels
  - 3 worked examples — one sample paragraph per channel rewritten in the source voice
  - "What we don't say" list — the performative language to retire
  - Handoff notes for your team and any external copywriters/designers

### Phase 4 — Integration Window (2 weeks, async)

- Two 60-min office-hours calls with you to stress-test the architecture as your team applies it
- One round of written feedback on a live grant narrative or website draft using the new voice
- Recorded 20-min voice memo summarizing the architecture for board presentation

## Why This Approach

Most brand refreshes solve the symptom — visual inconsistency, tone drift — by imposing a new surface. The dysregulation returns within a year because the underlying voice was never named. Say Why treats the organization's narrative as something to be *excavated* rather than constructed, then translated faithfully into each channel. That's why this is the methodology, not content strategy: the voice you end with is the one Delta Housing already has, now made legible to itself.

The camera, when we use it, is a co-regulatory witness — not an extraction device. Sessions are filmed only at your invitation, and footage is yours.
