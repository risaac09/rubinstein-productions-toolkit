## Understanding Your Situation

Here's what I'm hearing between the lines:

Commons Collective is in a stage of **narrative dysregulation through growth**. The organization is doing the real thing — mutual aid across four cities, eight staff, a field that resists commodification — and the outside-facing story is trying to keep up with what the inside-facing reality keeps becoming. Every new team member joins and needs the origin story re-told. Every funder requires a fresh translation of why this work, why now, why you. You're currently doing that translation yourself, which means the cost of growth is paid in your own re-explanation labor.

The pattern I see across mutual-aid infrastructure orgs at your stage: the founder's voice becomes a bottleneck not because anyone wants it to, but because no one else has been given the scaffolding to speak from the same depth. The story fragments across onboarding decks, grant narratives, site visits, and Instagram captions — each version slightly different, each one requiring re-regulation from you.

You don't need a rebrand. You need **a narrative stewardship function** — someone outside the org who holds the excavation, keeps the through-line coherent across quarters, and builds the internal scaffolding so new staff and funders can receive the story without requiring your direct transmission every time.

This is Territory work. It's not a one-time project because the story itself is alive and will keep moving as you grow. It's a partnership because the through-line has to be held by someone who has the bandwidth to metabolize what you're becoming in real time.

**What Territory Means:**
An ongoing Say Why partnership where I function as your external narrative steward. I hold the through-line, surface the patterns you can't see from inside, and translate the living work into language your team, funders, and public can receive without draining your own capacity to re-explain.

### Phase 1 — Excavation & Baseline (Month 1)

**Objective:** Establish the through-line in its current form.

- **Discovery intensive:** 3 x 90-minute sessions with you (Jamal) — narrative excavation, surfacing the non-obvious load-bearing stories
- **Staff listening sessions:** 4 x 60-minute conversations with your 8-person team (paired) — what story are they already telling, where does it diverge, where does it converge
- **Archive review:** Grant narratives, onboarding materials, public comms from the last 18 months — mapping narrative drift
- **Deliverable:** **Bilingual Dashboard v1** — a living document that holds your through-line in two registers: the inside version (how you actually talk about the work with each other) and the outside version (how it gets translated for funders, new staff, public). This is your narrative source-of-truth.

### Phase 2 — Stewardship Rhythm (Months 2–5)

**Objective:** Hold the through-line as it moves, build internal capacity for voice coherence.

Each month includes:
- **1 x 90-minute strategy session** with you — narrative check-in, surface what's shifting, metabolize quarterly growth
- **1 x 60-minute team session** — rotating focus: onboarding narrative, funder translation, public voice, program storytelling
- **2 x asynchronous narrative reviews** — I review a draft (grant, deck, campaign, announcement) and return it with Say Why edits that preserve your voice while tightening translation
- **Bilingual Dashboard updates** — the document stays alive; I version it as your story evolves
- **On-call narrative support** — Slack/email access for "how do we say this" moments, capped at reasonable turnaround (48hr async)

### Phase 3 — Through-Line Consolidation (Month 6)

**Objective:** Transfer the stewardship scaffolding so the org holds its own coherence.

- **Narrative coherence audit:** What's stable, what's still moving, what needs another cycle
- **Onboarding Voice Kit:** A distilled resource that lets new staff land in the story without requiring you to re-transmit it personally
- **Funder Translation Library:** Pre-metabolized language for the 3–4 funder archetypes Commons Collective is speaking to, so grant-writing no longer starts from zero
- **6-Month Strategic Debrief:** Whether to renew (Territory is renewable in 6-month increments), evolve the scope, or step back

### What You'll Have at Month 6

1. **Bilingual Dashboard (living document, versioned)** — your narrative source-of-truth
2. **Onboarding Voice Kit** — for new staff
3. **Funder Translation Library** — for grants and development
4. **An internal team fluent in the through-line** — so it's not held only by you
5. **A narrative stewardship rhythm your org knows how to run** — with or without me

### Zero-Measurement Zone (if filming/media capture enters scope)

## Why This Approach

Most communications consultants come in, run a discovery, deliver a messaging framework, and leave. Six months later the framework is stale because the organization has moved and the framework didn't.

Say Why is different because it assumes the story is alive. My job isn't to fix your message — it's to steward the through-line as your work moves. I'm not trying to make Commons Collective sound more polished. I'm trying to make sure the inside version and the outside version stay in honest relationship, so your team doesn't fragment and your funders receive the real thing.

This is what I mean by **the methodology**: the translation itself generates new coherence, rather than flattening what's actually happening. It's non-extractive because I'm not mining your story for content — I'm holding its shape so it can keep growing.

Mutual-aid infrastructure is one of the hardest things to translate publicly because it refuses the hero narrative that funders have been trained to reward. You need a narrative steward who understands that refusal is part of the work, not a comms problem to solve.
