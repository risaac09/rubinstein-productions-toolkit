## The Problem You Named

> "Our story changes every quarter as we grow, and we need someone who can hold the through-line without us having to re-explain ourselves every time we onboard a new team member or funder."

You're describing a specific kind of organizational tax: the cost of re-explaining yourselves. Every new staff member, every new funder conversation, every new city you enter, someone on your team has to reconstruct the story from scratch. The narrative drifts. The language fragments. The through-line gets lost in the growth.

Mutual-aid infrastructure work is especially vulnerable to this. The field evolves fast, your model adapts to each city's conditions, and the language of "infrastructure" is easy to flatten into jargon. Without a steward, the story becomes whatever the most recent conversation made it.

You don't need a one-time brand project. You need someone embedded enough to hold continuity as you change.

## What a Territory Engagement Actually Is

The Territory tier is an ongoing relationship, not a deliverable. It's designed for organizations whose story needs to live and breathe alongside the work — where narrative is infrastructure, not decoration.

I function as your **external narrative steward**: I hold the through-line across quarters, translate internal shifts into language that lands, and make sure new team members and funders walk into a coherent story rather than a fragmented one.

This is Voice Liberation applied at the organizational level. Your voice is already there. My job is to keep it legible to itself as you grow.

## Scope of the Engagement

### Ongoing (every month)

**1. Narrative Continuity Stewardship**
- Maintain a living "narrative source of truth" document that captures Commons Collective's core story, positioning, and language — updated quarterly as you evolve
- Hold the through-line across your 4 cities, so each chapter reads as part of one organization rather than four
- Flag drift early: when the language in a grant app, a board deck, and a job posting are pulling apart, I name it

**2. Onboarding Narrative Kit (Maintained)**
- A concise, evolving onboarding document new staff receive on day one — what Commons Collective is, why it exists, how we talk about it, and what makes us distinct
- Refreshed each quarter so new hires always get the current version, not last year's

**3. Funder-Facing Narrative Support**
- On-call narrative support for funder conversations: framing memos, story sharpening before big pitches, post-meeting debriefs to capture what landed
- Review of grant narratives, LOIs, and reports before they go out — not writing them, but making sure the through-line holds

**4. Monthly Working Sessions (2 x 90 min)**
- One session with you (Jamal) — strategic narrative check-in, upcoming decisions, what's shifting
- One session with a rotating team member or city lead — surfacing how the story is actually being told on the ground, and where it's drifting

**5. Quarterly Narrative Review**
- A deeper session each quarter: what changed, what needs to be re-articulated, what new language the organization is generating that should be captured

**6. Asynchronous Access**
- Reasonable async availability for narrative gut-checks: "does this land?", "how do we frame this?", "is this on-message?"
- Response within 1 business day

### Explicitly Out of Scope (for this tier)
- Full brand identity redesign or visual identity work
- Website copywriting or production
- Press/media relations or PR
- Copywriting for campaigns, fundraising appeals, or marketing collateral (can be added as scoped projects)
- Video production or content creation (separate engagement)

## Why This Fits Commons Collective

Mutual-aid infrastructure work is narrative-dependent in a way most nonprofits aren't. Your model is harder to explain than direct-service work. Your theory of change requires more unpacking. Every funder conversation is partly a translation exercise.

An external steward who holds the through-line frees you and your team from being the only people who can articulate the organization. That's leverage — especially as you grow beyond what any single founder-narrator can hold in their head.

I'm not here to put words in your mouth. I'm here to make sure the words already coming out of your mouth — and your team's mouths — stay coherent with each other as you scale.
