## What the Mirror Tier Is

The Mirror tier is a focused, 1:1 narrative excavation arc. Three sessions, held over four to six weeks, designed to surface the language that is already yours — not manufacture new language on top of it.

The premise: you don't need to *learn* how to talk about Bright Current. You already know. What you need is a process that helps you hear yourself clearly, distinguish borrowed language from native language, and come out with a handful of sentences you can actually stand behind in a funder meeting.

This isn't messaging strategy. It isn't positioning. It's narrative excavation — finding what's already true and getting it into words you recognize as yours.

---

## The Three-Session Arc

**Session 1 — Surface (90 minutes)**
We start with the territory you actually move in. I'll ask you to talk about Bright Current the way you'd talk about it to another climate scientist — no performance, no funder framing. I listen for the language that lands when you're in your own element. We'll also look at the "polished but hollow" version: what are you reaching for? Where did that language come from? What is it trying to protect?

Between sessions: light homework. Three short voice memos, unedited, responding to prompts I'll send.

**Session 2 — Excavate (90 minutes)**
We go into the material from session 1 and the voice memos. I reflect back patterns — the phrases that carry heat, the phrases that go flat, the places where you shift registers. This is the "mirror" part of Mirror tier: you hear yourself from the outside. We start drafting the sentences that belong to you — not taglines, but load-bearing language you can reuse.

Between sessions: you test the drafted language in one real context (a funder email, a partner conversation, a bio rewrite — your call).

**Session 3 — Integrate (90 minutes)**
We debrief what happened when you tested it. Where did it land? Where did you still reach for the hollow version? We refine. You leave with a short document — your voice, on paper, in language you recognize — that you can use as a reference when you're writing grants, pitching partners, or introducing yourself.

## What This Is Not
