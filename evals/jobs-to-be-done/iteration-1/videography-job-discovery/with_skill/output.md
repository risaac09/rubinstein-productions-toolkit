# What Job Is a Nonprofit ED Hiring You To Do?

You're not being paid to make a video. You're being paid to move a specific person from a current state to a better one, in a specific circumstance. The video is just the artifact that gets hired to do that work.

Let's unpack it through the three dimensions, then get to candidate job statements.

## The Three Dimensions of This Job

| Dimension | What the ED actually needs |
|-----------|----------------------------|
| **Functional** | Produce an asset that compresses the organization's story into something a board member, major donor, or foundation program officer can consume in 2-3 minutes and say "yes" to. Reduce the cost of explaining the org to strangers. |
| **Emotional** | Feel proud of the org when sharing it. Stop feeling embarrassed or apologetic about messaging that's been stale for 5 years. Feel *legitimized* — like a "real" organization that peers take seriously. Relieve the private anxiety of "are we even communicating what we actually do?" |
| **Social** | Be perceived by the board as a competent leader investing in the org's future. Be perceived by funders as professional and well-run. Be perceived by peer EDs at conferences as someone whose org is "having a moment." Signal to staff that leadership is investing in their work being seen. |

**If you ignore the emotional and social dimensions, you will make a technically good video that the ED still feels vaguely dissatisfied with** — because you solved the wrong job.

## Candidate Job Statements

Use the format: *When [circumstances], I want to [progress], so I can [outcome].*

Here are four likely jobs an ED might be hiring you for. The real job depends on circumstance — interview to find out which.

### Job 1: The Fundraising Unlock
> "When I'm walking into a major gift meeting or a foundation pitch, I want a 2-minute asset that does the emotional heavy lifting before I open my mouth, so I can spend the meeting on the ask instead of re-explaining who we are."

**Push:** Exhausted from telling the same origin story 200 times. Current pitch deck is flat.
**Pull:** A film that makes funders lean in and feel the mission in their body.
**Anxiety:** "What if it's cheesy? What if it looks like every other nonprofit video?"
**Habit:** Sending a PDF and hoping for the best.

### Job 2: The Board / Staff Alignment Tool
> "When my board and staff each describe what we do in five different ways, I want a shared artifact that we can all point to and say 'that's us,' so I can stop relitigating our identity in every meeting."

**Push:** Internal misalignment is draining leadership time.
**Pull:** A crystallized external mirror that unifies the story.
**Anxiety:** "What if the video picks a frame that alienates half my board?"
**Habit:** Strategic planning retreats that never quite land.

### Job 3: The Legitimacy / Peer Signal
> "When we're applying for larger grants and competing with bigger-name orgs, I want to look and sound like we belong at that table, so I can stop being underestimated."

**Push:** Being treated as a small/scrappy org when the work is sophisticated.
**Pull:** Production values that signal seriousness.
**Anxiety:** "Will funders think we wasted money on a video?"
**Habit:** Over-explaining credentials in narrative form.

### Job 4: The Founder/ED Legacy Moment
> "When I'm stepping into my 5th year / approaching a leadership transition / celebrating an anniversary, I want to capture this chapter of the org while it's alive, so I can hand something to whoever comes next."

**Push:** Mortality of institutional memory. Founder fatigue.
**Pull:** A document of this moment, this team, this voice.
**Anxiety:** "Will this feel self-aggrandizing?"
**Habit:** Meaning-making through internal storytelling only.

## What You're Really Being Paid For

Across all four jobs, the pattern is the same: **you are being hired to externalize something the ED already holds internally but cannot efficiently transmit.** The video is a compression algorithm for meaning. You're being paid for:

1. **Translation** — turning inside-knowledge into outside-legibility
2. **Legitimization** — the production values ARE part of the deliverable, because they signal credibility to third parties
3. **Emotional risk-absorption** — you hold the anxiety of "what if this is cringe" so the ED doesn't have to
4. **Decision acceleration** — every second you shave off a funder's "do I get it?" moment is the actual ROI

## The Competitive Landscape (Who Else Is "Hired" For This Job?)

This is where it gets useful. Your real competition isn't other videographers. It's:

- **Non-consumption** — the ED keeps using the deck and keeps hoping
- **A copywriter / brand strategist** who rewrites the one-liner
- **A photographer** for a rebrand photo shoot
- **An in-house junior staffer with an iPhone** (the "good enough" modular alternative)
- **A consultant-led strategic planning retreat** (same underlying job: alignment + clarity)
- **A PR firm** (same underlying job: legitimacy signaling)

When you understand you're competing against a strategic planning retreat AND a PR firm AND an iPhone, your positioning shifts. You're not the "video vendor." You're the person who does narrative externalization through moving image — and the artifact happens to also work as a fundraising tool.

## How To Find Out Which Job THIS ED Is Hiring For

Don't ask "what do you want the video to do?" They'll say "tell our story." That's a goal, not a job.

Instead, reconstruct the timeline (Mom Test + JTBD interview style):

1. **First thought:** "When did you first think 'we need a new video'? What happened that week?"
2. **Search:** "Who else did you consider? What made you rule them out?"
3. **Purchase moment:** "What finally made you reach out to me specifically?"
4. **Imagined usage:** "Walk me through the first time you'll show this to someone. Who is it? Where are you? What do you want them to do next?"
5. **Fear:** "What's the version of this video that would disappoint you?"

The answers tell you which job is actually being hired — and whether you're being hired for fundraising (Job 1), alignment (Job 2), legitimacy (Job 3), or legacy (Job 4). Scope, tone, run-time, and even shot list flow from that answer.

## The Voice Liberation Frame

Given your Voice Liberation methodology: the underlying job across all four statements is almost always **"help me (the ED) say the thing I'm already saying, but in a form that carries weight I can't give it alone."** You're not being hired to find the message. You're being hired to *externalize and amplify a voice that already exists inside the ED and the org* — and to absorb the vulnerability of letting that voice be seen.

That's the real deliverable. The video is the container.
